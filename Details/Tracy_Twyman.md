# Tracy Twyman
Independent researcher investigating Kappy's Epstein claims, found hanged in garage two months after Kappy's death.

## Basic Information
- **Full Name:** Tracy R. Twyman
- **Date of Death:** July 2019
- **Age at Death:** 41
- **Cause of Death:** Hanging
- **Location:** Her garage (exact city undisclosed)
- **Official Ruling:** Suicide (husband ruled out as suspect)
- **Suspicion Level:** SUSPICIOUS

## Connection to Epstein Network
Tracy Twyman was a podcast producer, author of approximately seven books on occult and esoteric history, and independent researcher. In 2018, she began investigating [Isaac Kappy](Isaac_Kappy.md)'s claims about pedophilia in Hollywood and connections to [Jeffrey Epstein](Jeffrey_Epstein.md)'s trafficking network. She was undeterred by Kappy's death in May 2019 and continued the research, focusing on networks she believed connected Hollywood, elite pedophilia rings, and the Epstein operation.

## Circumstances Raising Suspicion
- Found hanged — the same method as [Jeffrey Epstein](Jeffrey_Epstein.md), [Jean-Luc Brunel](Jean_Luc_Brunel.md), [Mark Middleton](Mark_Middleton.md), and [Thomas Bowers](Thomas_Bowers.md)
- Died approximately two months after [Isaac Kappy](Isaac_Kappy.md), whose research she was continuing
- On May 24, 2019 — just 11 days after Kappy's death — she recorded a 27-minute "dead man's switch" video given to two trusted friends for safekeeping
- In the video, she detailed threats against her life, described her computer being hacked, and drew connections between her case and Kappy's death
- She specifically mentioned a "creepy" Arizona campground connected to her research that was suddenly closed after she investigated it
- She contacted the FBI about her findings regarding pedophilia allegations, but the FBI showed no interest
- She had quit social media and YouTube shortly before her death — interpreted by some as a sign she was frightened
- Her death occurred approximately one month before Epstein's arrest in July 2019

## Context
Tracy Twyman's husband was investigated and ruled out as a suspect in her death. The official ruling was suicide by hanging. However, her pre-recorded warning video, the threats she documented, and the timing of her death — sandwiched between Kappy's death and Epstein's arrest — have made her a prominent figure in discussions of suspicious deaths connected to the Epstein case.

The "dead man's switch" video she left behind is frequently cited as evidence that she anticipated foul play. The video's contents describe an atmosphere of intimidation and surveillance that she believed was connected to her research into elite pedophilia networks.
