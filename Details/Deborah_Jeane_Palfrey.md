## Context
Palfrey was convicted on April 15, 2008, of racketeering, mail fraud, and money laundering in connection with running Pamela Martin and Associates from 1993 to 2006. She faced a potential 55-year sentence, with prosecutors estimating 57–71 months (approximately 6 years).

She had previously served 18 months in prison on unrelated charges and told journalist Dan Moldea: "I am not going back to prison. I will commit suicide first." However, she had also specifically said she would never hang herself, referencing the death of former employee Brandy Britton who had hanged herself in January 2007 before trial on prostitution charges.

Police found handwritten suicide notes dated approximately a week before her death. The autopsy and final police investigation concluded suicide.

Her death is relevant to the broader pattern because it demonstrates what can happen to individuals who possess compromising information about powerful Washington figures — the same kind of information [Jeffrey Epstein](Jeffrey_Epstein.md) allegedly collected through his trafficking and blackmail operation.
