## Context
Anne Heche was an actress known for roles in films and television spanning three decades. On August 5, 2022, she crashed her car into a two-story house in Mar Vista, Los Angeles, causing a massive fire. She was rescued from the burning vehicle but suffered severe burns and smoke inhalation. She was declared brain dead on August 11 and taken off life support on August 12.

The official ruling was accidental death. Toxicology reports found cocaine and marijuana in her system, indicating impairment at the time of the crash. Some reports also referenced fentanyl, though it may have been administered during hospital treatment. Her representatives initially denied drug use, but the coroner's report was definitive.

Heche had been open about being sexually abused by her father as a child and had spoken about the lasting trauma. She had a history of erratic public behavior attributed to mental health struggles stemming from that abuse.

No verified evidence connects her to any specific investigation of Epstein-linked networks. The online narrative appears to be based primarily on her role in *Girl in Room 13*, the timing of her death during a period of renewed Epstein-related scrutiny, and her personal history as an abuse survivor. The film itself was a standard Lifetime movie about trafficking, not an Epstein exposé.
