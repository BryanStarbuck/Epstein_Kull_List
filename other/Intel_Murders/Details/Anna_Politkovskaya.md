## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Six people were convicted in connection with Politkovskaya's murder, including former FSB officer Dmitry Pavlyuchenkov and former police officer Sergei Khadzhikurbanov. However, the person who ordered the killing has never been identified or prosecuted. The European Court of Human Rights found that Russia's investigation was inadequate. The FSB had her under surveillance and had previously targeted her email with malware. She was killed on Putin's birthday — widely interpreted as a "gift" to the president.

## Circumstances of Death

On October 7, 2006 — Vladimir Putin's 54th birthday — Anna Politkovskaya returned to her apartment building in central Moscow carrying groceries. As she entered the elevator, a gunman shot her at point-blank range. She was hit with four bullets, including one to the head that served as the coup de grace. A Makarov pistol and four spent cartridge cases were found near her body.

Security cameras recorded a young man in a baseball cap following her into the building, but the footage was of poor quality. The murder was immediately recognized as a contract killing.

## Background

Anna Politkovskaya was a Russian-American journalist, writer, and human rights activist who worked as a special correspondent for the independent newspaper *Novaya Gazeta*. She was one of the very few journalists to report extensively from inside Chechnya during the Second Chechen War, documenting atrocities committed by Russian forces and Chechen security services loyal to Ramzan Kadyrov.

Her reporting included:
- Forced disappearances and extrajudicial executions in Chechnya
- Torture in "filtration camps" run by Russian forces
- Civilian massacres by both sides of the conflict
- Corruption and human rights abuses by the Kadyrov government

She authored several books, including *A Small Corner of Hell: Dispatches from Chechnya* and *Putin's Russia: Life in a Failing Democracy*. She received numerous international awards for her journalism and human rights work.

Politkovskaya had survived a previous poisoning attempt in September 2004, when she fell ill after drinking tea on a flight to Beslan during the school hostage crisis. She lost consciousness and was hospitalized, with her colleagues believing the tea was poisoned to prevent her from reaching Beslan.

## Intelligence Connections

- **FSB surveillance:** Pavel Ryaguzov, a lieutenant colonel in the FSB, was arrested and accused of gathering intelligence on Politkovskaya's movements in preparation for the murder
- **FSB malware:** According to documents released by Edward Snowden, Russian intelligence targeted Politkovskaya's webmail account with malicious software in December 2005
- **Prior poisoning attempt:** The 2004 poisoning on the flight to Beslan bore hallmarks of an intelligence operation
- **Chechen connection:** The hitman, Rustam Makhmudov, and his brothers had ties to Chechen organized crime. The investigation pointed to links between the killers and Chechen security forces
- **Mastermind unknown:** Despite convictions, Russian authorities never identified who ordered and financed the contract killing
- **Pattern:** Politkovskaya was the third *Novaya Gazeta* journalist to be murdered since 2000
