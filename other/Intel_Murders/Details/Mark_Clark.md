## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Mark Clark was killed in the same FBI-coordinated COINTELPRO raid that killed Fred Hampton. Clark was shot through the heart. Ballistics experts determined that the single shot attributed to the Panthers' side — fired from Clark's shotgun — was likely a reflexive discharge as he was killed, not a deliberate shot. Clark was sitting in the front room of the apartment acting as a security guard when police burst through the door.

## Circumstances of Death

At approximately 4:30 AM on December 4, 1969, fourteen police officers from the Cook County State's Attorney's tactical unit raided Hampton's apartment. Mark Clark was sitting in the front room near the door, acting as a security lookout, when police kicked in the door and immediately opened fire.

Clark was shot through the heart. As he was hit, his shotgun discharged once — the only shot fired from the Panthers' side out of 82-99 total rounds. Forensic analysis indicated this was a reflexive discharge as Clark was killed, not a deliberate return of fire.

Police had been provided with a detailed floor plan of the apartment by FBI informant William O'Neal, including the locations where occupants were likely to be sleeping or stationed.

## Background

Mark Clark was born in Peoria, Illinois, the ninth child of Fannie Mae Clark and Pastor William Clark. He grew up in a religious household — his father founded the Holy Temple Church of God in Christ. Despite considerable talent in art, drama, and speech, Clark left school in the 8th grade due to the racially hostile environment facing Black students.

Clark became active with the NAACP in Peoria, which made him a target of the local police department. He was repeatedly beaten and detained on fabricated charges — a common practice in Peoria, where many police officers were reportedly members of the Ku Klux Klan.

In December 1968, Clark was introduced to the Black Panther Party through family friend Henry Howard. He traveled to Chicago, where he met Fred Hampton, and subsequently founded the Peoria chapter of the Black Panthers. Clark was instrumental in establishing the Free Breakfast Program in Peoria, organized free clothing and shoe giveaways for children, and was in the process of creating a free clinic and sickle cell anemia foundation at the time of his death.

Clark had traveled to Chicago the night of December 3, 1969, to work with Hampton on Panther organizing efforts.

## Intelligence Connections

* The raid was planned and coordinated by the FBI as part of COINTELPRO
* FBI informant William O'Neal provided the apartment floor plan to the raiding officers
* The FBI's stated goal under COINTELPRO was to "neutralize" Black Panther leaders
* Clark had been the target of police harassment in Peoria well before the raid
* No officer was ever criminally charged for Clark's death
* The Clark and Hampton families received a combined $1.85 million settlement in 1982 from the federal government, Cook County, and City of Chicago
