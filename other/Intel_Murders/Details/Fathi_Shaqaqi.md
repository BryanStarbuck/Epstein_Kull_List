## Assessment: CONFIRMED

The assassination is widely attributed to Mossad and has been acknowledged in Israeli intelligence histories. According to accounts published by Israeli journalists, Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin directly ordered Mossad chief Shabtai Shavit to kill Shaqaqi following the January 1995 Beit Lid suicide bombing that killed 22 Israelis. The Daily Telegraph's 2023 obituary of Mossad chief Shavit explicitly referenced the Malta operation. Malta's small size and neutral status made it an unusual and bold choice for an intelligence operation.

## Circumstances of Death

On October 26, 1995, Shaqaqi was walking outside the Diplomat Hotel in Sliema, Malta, when two men approached him on a motorcycle. One of the men shot him five times at close range, then the pair fled. Shaqaqi had been traveling under the false identity of "Dr. Ibrahim Ali Shawesh" and was returning from Tripoli, Libya, where he had met with Muammar Gaddafi, who had reportedly promised financial support for PIJ operations. Mossad had tracked his movements and waited for him to transit through Malta — a location where he would be outside the protection of any allied state.

## Background

Shaqaqi was a Palestinian physician born in Rafah in the Gaza Strip. He founded the Palestinian Islamic Jihad (PIJ) in the early 1980s, inspired by the Iranian Revolution and seeking to combine Palestinian nationalism with Islamic revolutionary ideology. He was deported by Israel to Lebanon in 1988 and eventually settled in Damascus, Syria, under the protection of the Assad regime. As PIJ leader, he directed a campaign of suicide bombings inside Israel during the early 1990s, most notably the Beit Lid junction attack on January 22, 1995, which killed 22 Israeli soldiers and civilians. This attack reportedly prompted Rabin to order his assassination.

## Intelligence Connections

* Mossad carried out the assassination using a two-man team on a motorcycle
* Israeli PM Rabin reportedly ordered Mossad chief Shavit to kill Shaqaqi immediately after the Beit Lid bombing
* Mossad concluded that Shaqaqi could not be killed in Damascus due to logistical difficulties and ongoing secret Israeli-Syrian negotiations
* The operation waited for Shaqaqi to travel to a vulnerable location outside Syrian protection
* Shaqaqi's use of a false identity was known to Mossad, indicating deep intelligence penetration of PIJ networks
* The Maltese government was not informed of the operation and had no prior knowledge

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- The assassination was carried out on the sovereign territory of Malta, a neutral country, without its knowledge or consent
- Shaqaqi was killed just weeks before the assassination of Israeli PM Rabin himself (November 4, 1995), who had ordered the operation
- The operation demonstrated Mossad's ability to track targets across multiple countries using false identities
- PIJ was weakened but not destroyed by Shaqaqi's death, raising questions about the strategic effectiveness of targeted killings
- The boldness of the operation — a daylight shooting outside a hotel in a small, peaceful European island — showed Mossad's willingness to act anywhere
- No one was ever arrested or prosecuted for the killing
