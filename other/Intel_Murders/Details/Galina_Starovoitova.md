## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Galina Starovoitova was the seventh Russian Duma deputy murdered since 1993, shot dead in her apartment building lobby while preparing to run for governor. Two gunmen were convicted, but the person who ordered the killing has never been conclusively identified. The assassination bore hallmarks of a professional contract killing with alleged connections between organized crime and Russia's security services.

## Circumstances of Death

On the evening of November 20, 1998, Starovoitova returned to her apartment building on the Griboedov Canal in St. Petersburg. She was accompanied by her aide, Ruslan Linkov. As they entered the lobby, gunmen opened fire. Starovoitova was hit in the head, neck, and chest and died on the spot. Linkov was severely wounded but survived.

The assassins fled the building. A Kalashnikov-type weapon and a pistol were reportedly found at the scene. The killing had all the hallmarks of a professional contract hit — the attackers were waiting inside the building and struck quickly.

## Background

Starovoitova was a Soviet-era ethnographer who became one of Russia's most prominent pro-democracy politicians. She was elected to the Congress of People's Deputies of the Soviet Union in 1989 as a representative from Armenia and became a member of the reformist Inter-Regional Group alongside Andrei Sakharov and Boris Yeltsin. In 1991, she served as Yeltsin's spokesperson during his presidential campaign and later became his advisor on interethnic affairs.

By 1998, Starovoitova was an outspoken critic of communists, extreme nationalists, and organized crime's infiltration of Russian politics. She had proposed legislation requiring officials to disclose KGB ties. At the time of her death, she had announced plans to run for governor of Leningrad Oblast and had reportedly spoken of running for president in 2000.

## Intelligence Connections

* Starovoitova had proposed a "lustration" law that would have exposed former KGB officers in government — a direct threat to the security establishment
* Businessman Vladimir Barsukov (Kumarin), alleged head of the Tambov organized crime group, was reportedly charged with ordering the assassination after Starovoitova blocked his attempts to establish ties with corrupt city officials
* The Tambov group allegedly had connections to elements within Russia's security services
* Multiple Russian intelligence and security figures reportedly opposed Starovoitova's anti-corruption and transparency initiatives

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- She was the sixth or seventh Duma member murdered since 1993, reflecting a pattern of targeted political killings
- Her proposed legislation to expose former KGB agents in government gave security services direct motive
- Two triggermen were convicted (Vitaly Akishin sentenced to 23.5 years; Yury Kolchin to 20 years in 2005), but the person who ordered the assassination was never conclusively identified
- Starovoitova was buried as a national hero — a kilometer-long line of mourners stood in bitter cold to attend her public viewing
- Her murder contributed to the silencing of liberal democratic voices in the late 1990s, just as Vladimir Putin was ascending to power
