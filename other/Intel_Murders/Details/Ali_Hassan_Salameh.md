## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Ali Hassan Salameh was killed by a Mossad car bomb on January 22, 1979, as the final mission of Operation Wrath of God — Israel's covert assassination campaign targeting those responsible for the 1972 Munich Olympics massacre. The operation is well-documented in multiple sources. Notably, Salameh was simultaneously serving as a CIA asset and the primary back-channel between the United States and the PLO, making his assassination a point of significant tension between American and Israeli intelligence.

## Circumstances of Death

On January 22, 1979, Salameh was traveling in a convoy of two Chevrolet station wagons from his girlfriend's apartment to his mother's home for a birthday celebration. At 3:35 PM, as the convoy passed a parked Volkswagen on Rue Verdun in Beirut, a Mossad operative remotely detonated approximately 100 kilograms of plastic explosive packed inside the vehicle. Salameh was critically wounded and died on the operating table at the American University of Beirut Hospital. Four of his bodyguards were also killed in the blast, along with several bystanders including a British student and a German nun.

## Background

Salameh was the son of Sheikh Hassan Salameh, a Palestinian nationalist leader killed fighting Israeli forces in 1948. He became the chief of operations for the Black September Organization and was allegedly one of the planners of the September 1972 Munich Olympics attack that killed 11 Israeli athletes. He later founded Force 17, Yasser Arafat's elite personal security detail, and became head of PLO intelligence operations. Despite his role in Black September, Salameh developed a secret relationship with the CIA, serving as the primary conduit between the United States and the PLO. He reportedly ensured the safety of American citizens in Beirut during the Lebanese Civil War and provided intelligence on threats to U.S. interests.

## Intelligence Connections

* Mossad targeted Salameh as part of Operation Wrath of God, authorized by Prime Minister Golda Meir in 1972
* The CIA maintained Salameh as an asset and back-channel to the PLO leadership
* The CIA reportedly sent Salameh dozens of warnings about Mossad's intention to assassinate him
* The CIA provided Salameh with encrypted communications equipment and reportedly considered sending him an armored car
* A previous Mossad attempt to kill Salameh in Lillehammer, Norway in 1973 resulted in the mistaken killing of an innocent Moroccan waiter, Ahmed Bouchiki — one of Mossad's most embarrassing failures
* After the Lillehammer debacle, Salameh reportedly felt protected by his CIA relationship
