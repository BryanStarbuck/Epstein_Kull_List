## Assessment: CONFIRMED

This is one of the most clearly documented cases of a state-sponsored assassination on American soil. Chilean secret police (DINA) agents planted a bomb under Letelier's car, killing him and his 25-year-old American colleague Ronni Moffitt. The DINA operatives — including American-born agent Michael Townley — were convicted. DINA chief Manuel Contreras and his deputy Pedro Espinoza were convicted in Chile. Declassified documents show Secretary of State Henry Kissinger blocked a State Department warning to Chile that could have prevented the assassination.

## Circumstances of Death

On the morning of September 21, 1976, Orlando Letelier left his home in Bethesda, Maryland, with two colleagues from the Institute for Policy Studies: Ronni Karpen Moffitt, his 25-year-old American research assistant, and her husband Michael Moffitt. As they drove along Embassy Row in Letelier's blue Chevrolet Chevelle, approaching Sheridan Circle at approximately 9:35 AM, a bomb attached to the underside of the car detonated.

The explosion ripped through the car, severing Letelier's legs. He died at the scene. Ronni Moffitt suffered a severed carotid artery from shrapnel and drowned in her own blood. Michael Moffitt survived with injuries.

The bomb was a remote-controlled device containing C-4 plastic explosive, attached to the car's chassis with magnets. It was detonated by a radio signal.

## Background

Orlando Letelier was born in Temuco, Chile, and graduated from the Universidad de Chile with degrees in law and economics. Under President Salvador Allende, he served as Chile's ambassador to the United States (1971-1973), then as foreign minister and briefly as defense minister.

After the September 11, 1973 coup, Letelier was arrested by the Pinochet regime and imprisoned for a year at a concentration camp on Dawson Island in Chile's far south, where he was tortured. After international pressure secured his release in 1974, he moved to Washington and became a senior fellow at the Institute for Policy Studies, where he emerged as one of the most effective international critics of the Pinochet dictatorship.

Letelier was particularly effective at lobbying European governments and international organizations to impose sanctions on Chile and isolate the Pinochet regime diplomatically. This made him a prime target.

## Intelligence Connections

* The assassination was ordered by DINA (Direccion de Inteligencia Nacional), Chile's secret police, under the command of General Manuel Contreras.
* The bomb was built and planted by Michael Townley, an American-born DINA agent who entered the US on a forged Paraguayan passport.
* Townley recruited Cuban exile anti-Castro militants (CORU members) to assist with surveillance and logistics.
* The operation was part of **Operation Condor**, the coordinated campaign of political repression and assassination by South American dictatorships with CIA knowledge and support.
* Declassified documents reveal that a State Department communique warning Chile against carrying out assassinations was **blocked by Secretary of State Henry Kissinger**.
* The CIA had intelligence about Condor's assassination capabilities but failed to act on it or warn Letelier.
* General Pinochet, though never tried for the murders, was implicated by evidence as having ordered the assassination.
