# Frank Teruggi
American student and journalist, seized by Chilean military forces and executed at the National Stadium in Santiago during the 1973 Pinochet coup. A Chilean court confirmed that U.S. military intelligence provided information that led to his arrest and death.

## Assessment: CONFIRMED

U.S. intelligence complicity in Teruggi's death is confirmed by declassified documents and Chilean court rulings. In 1999, President Clinton ordered the release of documents admitting that U.S. intelligence agents played a role in the deaths of Teruggi and Charles Horman. In 2014, a Chilean court found that the United States played a "key role" in Teruggi's murder. In 2016, Chile's Supreme Court sentenced former DINA chief Pedro Espinoza to 15 years for the killing.

## Circumstances of Death

On September 20, 1973, nine days after the military coup that overthrew President Salvador Allende, Frank Teruggi was seized by Chilean military forces at his home in Santiago. He was taken to the National Stadium, which had been converted into a mass detention center where thousands of political prisoners were held, interrogated, tortured, and executed. Teruggi was executed on or around September 22, 1973. His body was found at the city morgue. He had been shot. The Chilean military provided no explanation for his detention or killing, and U.S. Embassy officials made only minimal efforts to locate him.

## Background

Teruggi was a young American from Chicago, Illinois, studying in Chile. He was a member of the Industrial Workers of the World (IWW) and worked as a freelance journalist, contributing to the English-language publication *FIN* (Fuente de Informacion Norteamericana), which reported on Chilean politics for North American readers. He had gone to Chile drawn by Allende's democratic socialist experiment. He was not involved in any military or violent activity — he was a student journalist documenting political events.

## Intelligence Connections

* Former U.S. Navy Captain Ray E. Davis, operating covert intelligence operations out of the U.S. Embassy in Santiago, reportedly provided information about Teruggi to Chilean military officials
* Davis was investigating Americans in Chile as part of intelligence operations targeting those considered "subversives or radicals"
* The information Davis provided to Chilean authorities allegedly led directly to Teruggi's arrest and execution
* Declassified documents released in 1999 confirmed U.S. intelligence involvement in Teruggi's death
* Chilean Judge Jorge Zepeda found in 2014 that U.S. military intelligence played a "key role" in both Teruggi's and Horman's deaths

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- A 24-year-old American student journalist was executed by a foreign military, with his own government's intelligence services providing the targeting information
- U.S. Embassy officials failed to protect an American citizen and may have actively facilitated his death
- The U.S. government concealed its role for over 25 years until documents were declassified in 1999
- No American official was ever criminally charged despite confirmed complicity
- Teruggi was targeted for his journalism and political views, not for any violent activity
- The Chilean military officers responsible received relatively lenient initial sentences, though these were later increased on appeal
