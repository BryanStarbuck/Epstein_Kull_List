## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

Zakharchenko's assassination remains contested, with multiple parties accused. Russia blamed Ukrainian intelligence, Ukraine blamed internal separatist power struggles or Russian FSB purges of unreliable proxy leaders. The sophistication of the bomb placement in a cafe he regularly visited suggests intelligence-level planning and inside knowledge of his movements.

## Circumstances of Death

On 31 August 2018, Zakharchenko entered the cafe "Separ" on Pushkin Boulevard in central Donetsk, a restaurant he was known to frequent. A bomb detonated inside the establishment, killing him and wounding several others, including DPR finance minister Alexander Timofeyev. The bomb had reportedly been placed in advance, indicating surveillance and planning by the perpetrators. The cafe was known as a regular gathering spot for DPR officials, making it both predictable and accessible to anyone with knowledge of Zakharchenko's schedule. Reports suggested the bomb was concealed in the lighting fixture above the area where Zakharchenko typically sat.

## Background

Zakharchenko was born in Donetsk and worked as a mine electrician before entering politics. In December 2013, he became head of the Donetsk branch of OPLOT, a pro-Russian militant organization. Following the Euromaidan revolution, he participated in the April 2014 seizure of Donetsk City Council offices. He succeeded Alexander Borodai as DPR prime minister on 7 August 2014 — Borodai later admitted Zakharchenko was installed as part of a Russian government effort to make the uprising appear to be a grassroots phenomenon. In the November 2014 DPR elections, Zakharchenko reportedly won with 78.93% of the vote in elections not recognized internationally.

## Intelligence Connections

* Ukraine's SBU (Security Service) presented two theories: the attack was organized by Russia's FSB as part of an ongoing purge of separatist leadership, or it was the result of personal score-settling among separatist factions over financial and criminal matters
* Russia accused Ukrainian intelligence of carrying out the assassination, calling it a "dastardly" crime aimed at destabilizing the Minsk peace process
* The sophistication of the bomb placement suggested professional intelligence tradecraft
* Multiple separatist leaders had been killed in similar attacks in both the DPR and LPR, suggesting a pattern of targeted eliminations
* Zakharchenko's security detail was reportedly penetrated or compromised, as the attackers had access to the cafe's interior
* The attack occurred during a period of increased tensions between Zakharchenko and Moscow-backed figures over control of DPR resources

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- The bomb was pre-planted in a cafe he frequented, requiring detailed knowledge of his habits and access to the location
- Both Ukrainian and Russian intelligence services had potential motives — Ukraine to eliminate a separatist leader, Russia to replace an increasingly independent proxy
- DPR finance minister Timofeyev, wounded in the same blast, had reportedly been in a power struggle with Zakharchenko
- The attack followed a pattern of separatist leader assassinations in Donbas, including motorola commander Arsen Pavlov (2016) and LPR leader Igor Plotnitsky's removal (2017)
- No group credibly claimed responsibility
- Zakharchenko had reportedly survived at least two previous assassination attempts
- His successor, Denis Pushilin, was installed within days, suggesting preparations had been made in advance
- The Donbas region remained a black hole for independent investigation, with no credible judicial process possible
