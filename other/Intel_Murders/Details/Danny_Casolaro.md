## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Danny Casolaro was found dead in a hotel bathtub with his wrists deeply slashed 10-12 times — an unusually violent method requiring extraordinary determination. He had repeatedly warned friends and family that if anything happened to him, it would not be suicide. His briefcase containing key documents was missing from the hotel room. The room was cleaned by a professional crew before a proper investigation. Casolaro had been investigating a sprawling conspiracy he called "The Octopus," connecting the theft of PROMIS software by the Department of Justice, CIA covert operations, organized crime, and the BCCI banking scandal. He was in Martinsburg to meet a source he believed would provide the final piece of his investigation.

## Circumstances of Death

On the morning of August 10, 1991, a maid at the Sheraton Hotel in Martinsburg, West Virginia, discovered Casolaro's body in the bathtub of Room 517. He was lying in a tub of bloody water. His wrists had been slashed deeply, 10-12 times, with what appeared to be a razor blade or box cutter (an Emory board with a razor blade embedded in it was later found).

Key suspicious elements:
- **The briefcase was missing.** Casolaro had been carrying a thick accordion-style briefcase containing his research files, interview notes, and documents related to "The Octopus." The briefcase was not found in his hotel room.
- **The room was cleaned.** The hotel room was cleaned by a professional crew the day after his death, before investigators could thoroughly process the scene. One crew member later recalled seeing two bloody towels in the bathroom, suggesting someone had attempted to clean blood off the floor before the professional crew arrived.
- **The body was embalmed.** Despite the family's explicit request for an autopsy before embalming, the body was embalmed quickly, making further forensic analysis more difficult.
- **His warnings.** Before his death, Casolaro had told friends, family members, and associates: "If anything happens to me, don't believe it was a suicide." He specifically warned his brother that if he was ever found dead, it would be murder.

## Background

Danny Casolaro was a freelance writer from Fairfax, Virginia, who had spent the last year of his life investigating what he called "The Octopus" — a sprawling network connecting:

1. **The INSLAW Affair:** The theft of PROMIS (Prosecutor's Management Information System) software by the U.S. Department of Justice from INSLAW, the company that developed it. PROMIS was allegedly modified by intelligence agencies to include a backdoor and then sold to foreign governments as a surveillance tool.

2. **CIA covert operations:** Connections between intelligence operatives, arms dealers, and drug trafficking networks.

3. **The October Surprise:** Allegations that the 1980 Reagan campaign conspired with Iran to delay the release of American hostages until after the election.

4. **BCCI:** The Bank of Credit and Commerce International, which was used for money laundering, intelligence operations, and arms trafficking.

5. **Organized crime connections:** Links between intelligence agencies and organized crime networks.

Casolaro believed these seemingly separate scandals were connected through a single network of intelligence operatives, government officials, and criminals — "The Octopus."

In 1990, Casolaro met Bill Hamilton, founder of INSLAW, who gave him a 12-page memo written by Michael Riconosciuto detailing allegations about PROMIS. Riconosciuto, a tech consultant who allegedly modified PROMIS for the CIA, became one of Casolaro's key sources.

## Intelligence Connections

* Casolaro's investigation centered on the alleged theft of PROMIS software by the DOJ and its distribution by the CIA to foreign intelligence services with a built-in surveillance backdoor
* Key source Michael Riconosciuto claimed to have modified PROMIS software for the CIA at the Cabazon Indian Reservation in California — a location allegedly used for weapons testing
* Casolaro was investigating connections between the PROMIS affair, Iran-Contra figures, and organized crime
* Multiple sources Casolaro was working with had intelligence connections or were themselves former intelligence operatives
* The missing briefcase suggested someone with knowledge of the investigation removed the documents
* Casolaro had reportedly told associates he was close to a breakthrough and was meeting a key source in Martinsburg

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Casolaro explicitly warned friends and family not to believe it if he was found dead and it was called suicide
- His briefcase containing key research documents vanished from the hotel room
- The hotel room was cleaned before investigators could process the scene
- The body was embalmed before the family could request an autopsy, against their wishes
- 10-12 deep slashes to the wrists is an unusually violent and determined method — inconsistent with typical suicide
- Two bloody towels suggested someone else had been in the room
- Casolaro was reportedly meeting a key source in Martinsburg and believed he was close to completing his investigation
- The investigation he was pursuing involved powerful intelligence and government figures
- A 2024 Netflix documentary series, *American Conspiracy: The Octopus Murders*, re-examined the case and brought renewed attention to the suspicious circumstances
