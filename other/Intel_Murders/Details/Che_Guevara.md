## Assessment: CONFIRMED

CIA involvement in the tracking, capture, and killing of Che Guevara is extensively documented through declassified U.S. government records. CIA operatives trained the Bolivian Rangers who captured Guevara, and CIA agent Felix Rodriguez was present at his execution, having relayed the Bolivian high command's order to kill him. While the execution order came from the Bolivian government, the entire operation was facilitated by CIA intelligence, training, and personnel.

## Circumstances of Death

On October 8, 1967, Guevara was captured by U.S.-trained Bolivian Army Rangers in a ravine near La Higuera. He was wounded in the leg during the firefight. He was taken to the schoolhouse in La Higuera and held overnight. CIA operative Felix Rodriguez interrogated Guevara and photographed his diary. Rodriguez received orders from the Bolivian high command that Guevara was to be executed. According to Rodriguez, he attempted to persuade Bolivian Colonel Joaquin Zenteno Anaya to allow Guevara to be transferred to CIA custody in Panama, but Anaya insisted the execution order be carried out. At approximately 1:10 p.m. on October 9, 1967, Bolivian Sergeant Mario Teran entered the schoolhouse and shot Guevara multiple times. His hands were amputated for fingerprint verification, and his body was buried in a secret location near Vallegrande, where it was not discovered until 1997.

## Background

Guevara was an Argentine physician turned revolutionary who played a central role in the Cuban Revolution alongside Fidel Castro. After helping establish Cuba's communist government, he served as president of the National Bank and Minister of Industries. He departed Cuba in 1965 to foment revolution abroad, first in the Congo and then in Bolivia, where he attempted to lead a guerrilla insurgency against the military government. His Bolivian campaign was plagued by lack of local support, difficult terrain, and effective counterinsurgency operations by the CIA-trained Bolivian Rangers.

## Intelligence Connections

* The CIA trained the Bolivian 2nd Ranger Battalion specifically to hunt Guevara's guerrilla force
* CIA operative Felix Rodriguez (codename "Felix Ramos") was embedded with the Bolivian military and was one of the last people to speak with Guevara before his execution
* Rodriguez relayed the execution order from the Bolivian high command, though he later claimed he wanted to keep Guevara alive
* Declassified National Security Archive documents confirm intensive CIA involvement in tracking Guevara's movements
* The highest levels of the White House, including National Security Advisor Walt Rostow, were closely monitoring the operation and were "intensely interested" in confirming Guevara's death

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- The Bolivian government initially claimed Guevara was killed in combat, which was a deliberate falsehood
- The decision to execute rather than try Guevara suggests the Bolivian and U.S. governments feared a public trial
- Guevara's hands were amputated and his burial site was kept secret for 30 years
- The U.S. government used his death to undermine leftist revolutionary movements across Latin America
- Questions remain about whether Washington explicitly endorsed the execution order or merely allowed it to proceed
- Rodriguez kept Guevara's Rolex watch as a personal trophy, which he wore publicly for decades
- The CIA's role extended beyond advisory — its operatives were embedded in the field unit that captured Guevara
- Guevara's remains were hidden for 30 years until a team located and exhumed them near Vallegrande in 1997
