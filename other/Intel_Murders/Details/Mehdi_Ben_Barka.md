## Assessment: CONFIRMED

A 1967 French preliminary trial established that Moroccan intelligence orchestrated the kidnapping in collusion with French police and criminals. Multiple intelligence services were involved: Moroccan Interior Minister Mohammed Oufkir reportedly participated directly; French SDECE provided logistical support; and Israeli Mossad allegedly located Ben Barka and assisted in planning. The case has been open since 1975 and remains the oldest unresolved case in France.

## Circumstances of Death

On October 29, 1965, Ben Barka was lured to a meeting outside the Brasserie Lipp on Boulevard Saint-Germain in Paris, reportedly under the pretense of discussing a film project. He was seized by French police officers and driven to a villa in Fontenay-le-Vicomte, south of Paris, owned by a criminal who also worked for the SDECE (French intelligence). At the villa, he was reportedly interrogated by Moroccan officials including Interior Minister Mohammed Oufkir. Ben Barka was killed during or after the interrogation. In 2001, former Moroccan secret service agent Ahmed Boukhari claimed that Ben Barka died during interrogation and that his body was taken back to Morocco and dissolved in a vat of acid. His body has never been found.

## Background

Ben Barka was a Moroccan mathematician, politician, and revolutionary who became the leading opposition figure against King Hassan II. He was a key architect of Moroccan independence, served as president of the National Consultative Assembly, and tutored the future King Hassan II in mathematics as a young man. After falling out with the monarchy, he was sentenced to death in absentia and lived in exile. He became a prominent figure in the Third World movement and was organizing the Tricontinental Conference in Havana at the time of his disappearance, which would have united anti-colonial movements from Africa, Asia, and Latin America.

## Intelligence Connections

* Moroccan intelligence orchestrated the kidnapping, with Interior Minister Mohammed Oufkir reportedly present at the villa
* French SDECE provided the villa and logistical support through its criminal networks
* French police officers physically seized Ben Barka on a Paris street
* According to Israeli intelligence sources, the Mossad located Ben Barka on behalf of Moroccan intelligence and assisted in planning the operation, reportedly as part of broader Israeli-Moroccan intelligence cooperation
* The CIA was reportedly "intimately aware of the plot as it was being prepared and carried out," according to a retired Moroccan police agent's revelations
* Human Rights Watch called on both the U.S. and France to release classified files on the case

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Four intelligence services from four countries allegedly collaborated in the operation
- Ben Barka's body has never been found, preventing definitive forensic conclusions
- The case has remained open in France for over 60 years — the longest unsolved case in French judicial history
- The affair caused a major diplomatic crisis between France and Morocco, with relations severed for nearly four years
- French President de Gaulle was reportedly furious that French police and intelligence acted on behalf of a foreign government on French soil
- The Mossad's involvement reportedly stemmed from Israeli-Moroccan intelligence cooperation on monitoring Moroccan Jewish emigration
- Key witnesses have died, been silenced, or fled before testifying
- French courts have reopened the investigation multiple times, most recently seeking access to CIA and DGSE archives
