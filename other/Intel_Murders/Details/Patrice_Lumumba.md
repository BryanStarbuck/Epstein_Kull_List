## Assessment: CONFIRMED

The CIA's role in plotting Lumumba's assassination was confirmed by the Church Committee in 1975. CIA Director Allen Dulles authorized a poison plot, and CIA scientist Sidney Gottlieb personally delivered a vial of poison to the Congo. Belgian intelligence officers directly participated in the execution. In 2002, Belgium formally apologized for its "moral responsibility" in the assassination. A 2001 Belgian parliamentary inquiry confirmed Belgian involvement in detail.

## Circumstances of Death

On January 17, 1961, Lumumba, along with two associates -- Maurice Mpolo and Joseph Okito -- were taken from prison in Elizabethville and driven to a remote location. They were executed by a firing squad composed of Katangan soldiers under Belgian officers' command, between 21:40 and 21:43 according to the Belgian parliamentary inquiry. Katangan leader Moise Tshombe, two other ministers, and four Belgian officers were present.

To destroy the evidence, Belgian Gendarmerie officer Gerard Soete and his team exhumed the bodies, dismembered them, and dissolved the remains in sulfuric acid. The bones were ground up and scattered. Soete later kept two of Lumumba's teeth as souvenirs. In 2022, Belgium returned one of Lumumba's teeth to his family.

## Background

Lumumba was a charismatic Congolese independence leader who became the first democratically elected Prime Minister when the Congo gained independence from Belgium on June 30, 1960. He was 35 years old.

Within days of independence, the Congolese army mutinied, and the mineral-rich Katanga Province seceded under Moise Tshombe, backed by Belgian mining company Union Miniere and Belgian intelligence. When Lumumba turned to the Soviet Union for assistance after the West refused to help, he was instantly labeled a communist threat during the Cold War.

- The Church Committee confirmed CIA assassination plots against Lumumba in its 1975 report
