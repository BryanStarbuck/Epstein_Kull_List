# Berta Caceres
Honduran indigenous Lenca leader and environmental activist assassinated in her home in 2016. A US-trained military intelligence officer was convicted of orchestrating her murder. She had been a prominent critic of the US-backed 2009 Honduran coup.

## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Honduran courts convicted David Castillo, a US-trained military intelligence officer turned energy company president, of orchestrating Caceres's assassination. Seven other men, including active and former military personnel, were also convicted. Evidence showed that Castillo used paid informants and military contacts to surveil Caceres and coordinated the logistics and financing of the hit. Multiple perpetrators received training at US military institutions, including West Point and the School of the Americas (now WHINSEC).

## Circumstances of Death

In the early hours of March 2, 2016, gunmen broke into the home where Berta Caceres was staying in La Esperanza, western Honduras. She was shot multiple times. Mexican environmentalist Gustavo Castro Soto, who was staying with Caceres, was also shot and wounded but survived by feigning death. He later became a key witness. The assassins fled the scene. Caceres had repeatedly reported death threats to Honduran authorities and international organizations, but no adequate protection was provided.

## Background

Berta Caceres was a co-founder and coordinator of the Civic Council of Popular and Indigenous Organizations of Honduras (COPINH), established in 1993 to defend Lenca indigenous rights, territory, and natural resources. She led a years-long campaign against the Agua Zarca hydroelectric dam on the Gualcarque River, sacred to the Lenca people. The dam was being developed by Desarrollos Energeticos S.A. (DESA), a Honduran company with ties to the country's military and business elite. In 2015, Caceres won the prestigious Goldman Environmental Prize for her activism. She was also a vocal critic of the 2009 US-backed military coup that ousted President Manuel Zelaya, and she documented the subsequent crackdown on civil liberties and indigenous rights.

## Intelligence Connections

* David Castillo, convicted as the mastermind, was a graduate of the US Military Academy at West Point and a former Honduran military intelligence officer before becoming president of DESA
* Major Mariano Diaz, one of the convicted hitmen, received special military training in the United States
* Douglas Giovanny Bustillo, another convicted perpetrator and former military officer, also trained in the United States
* A military whistleblower reported that Caceres's name was on a hit list maintained by Honduras's Inter-Institutional Security Force (FUSINA), which received training from US Marines and FBI agents
* Court proceedings revealed that Castillo used his military intelligence training and contacts to organize surveillance of Caceres in the months before her murder
* The Honduran military intelligence apparatus that enabled the assassination was substantially built and trained by the United States

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Multiple perpetrators were trained at US military institutions, raising questions about the accountability of US military training programs
- Caceres had formally requested protective measures from the Inter-American Commission on Human Rights, which Honduras failed to implement
- DESA's board included members of some of Honduras's most powerful military and business families
- International development banks, including the Dutch development bank FMO, funded the Agua Zarca project despite documented human rights concerns
- The killing followed the pattern of the post-2009 coup environment, in which over 100 environmental and land defenders were killed in Honduras
- Honduras had the highest per-capita murder rate of environmental activists in the world at the time of Caceres's killing
