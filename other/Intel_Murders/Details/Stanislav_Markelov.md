## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Stanislav Markelov was murdered less than 800 meters from the Kremlin while leaving a press conference about Chechen war crimes. While two neo-Nazi operatives were convicted, the broader question of state complicity — given Markelov's persistent challenges to the FSB and Russian military — has never been resolved. He had reportedly received threats from a high-ranking FSB general shortly before his death.

## Circumstances of Death

On 19 January 2009, Markelov left a press conference at the Independent Press Center in central Moscow where he had announced plans to challenge the early release of Colonel Yuri Budanov before the Russian Supreme Court. Budanov had been convicted of kidnapping and strangling 18-year-old Chechen woman Elza Kungaeva, and Markelov served as attorney for the Kungaeva family.

As Markelov walked down the street, Yevgenia Khasis followed him and signaled to Nikita Tikhonov, who approached from behind and shot Markelov in the back of the head with a silenced pistol. Freelance journalist Anastasia Baburova, who had attended the press conference and was walking alongside Markelov, attempted to pursue the attacker and was also shot in the head. She died hours later at a Moscow hospital.

## Background

Markelov was one of Russia's most prominent human rights lawyers. He represented victims of police violence, political activists, antifascist organizers, journalists, and Chechen civilians. His cases directly challenged the Russian military and security establishment, including representing the families of murdered Chechen civilians and defending journalists targeted for their reporting.

He had previously represented journalist Anna Politkovskaya and had taken on cases involving neo-Nazi violence, military brutality in Chechnya, and police torture. His work made him a target from multiple directions — both from ultra-nationalist groups he investigated and from the security services whose crimes he exposed in court.

## Intelligence Connections

* According to journalist Grigory Pasko, Markelov received threats from a high-ranking FSB general, Alexander Mikhailov, shortly before his death
* Former FSB officer Alexander Litvinenko (himself assassinated in 2006) had reportedly identified Markelov as a target of state security interest
* The neo-Nazi group BORN (Battle Organisation of Russian Nationalists), whose members killed Markelov, allegedly operated with a degree of state tolerance — critics have argued that such militant organizations served as useful proxies for eliminating troublesome activists
* Mikhail Trepashkin, a former FSB officer, stated he believed Markelov was murdered because he was a leader of opposition efforts that openly challenged FSB authority

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Murdered less than 800 meters from the Kremlin in broad daylight
- Killed on the same day he publicly announced plans to challenge the FSB-connected early release of Budanov
- Had received threats reportedly from a senior FSB official
- While neo-Nazis were convicted, the question of who directed or facilitated the targeting remains open
- The Russian Supreme Court quashed the convictions in December 2021 following a European Court of Human Rights ruling that the trial was unfair
- Pattern of Russian human rights lawyers and journalists being killed with impunity suggests systemic tolerance
