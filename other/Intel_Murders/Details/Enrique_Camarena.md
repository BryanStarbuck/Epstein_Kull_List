# Enrique "Kiki" Camarena
DEA special agent kidnapped, tortured for over 30 hours, and murdered in Guadalajara, Mexico, in 1985 after infiltrating the Guadalajara Cartel. CIA allegedly had foreknowledge and assets involved in the operation.

## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

While the cartel's direct role in Camarena's murder is well established, the alleged involvement of the CIA transforms this case from a narco-killing into a potential intelligence service betrayal. Multiple former DEA agents and investigators have claimed that CIA operatives not only knew about the kidnapping plot but had assets present during Camarena's interrogation and torture, allegedly because Camarena had discovered CIA-cartel connections to fund the Nicaraguan Contras.

## Circumstances of Death

On February 7, 1985, Camarena was abducted in broad daylight by five armed men as he left the U.S. Consulate in Guadalajara. He was taken to a residence owned by drug lord Rafael Caro Quintero. Over the next 30-plus hours, Camarena was brutally interrogated and tortured. A doctor was reportedly summoned to administer drugs to keep him conscious during the beatings. His skull, jaw, nose, cheekbones, and windpipe were crushed, his ribs were broken, and a hole was bored into his skull with a power drill. His body was not discovered until March 5, 1985, dumped in a field outside Guadalajara.

## Background

Camarena was born in Mexicali, Mexico, and grew up in a large family before moving to the United States. He served in the U.S. Marines before joining the DEA. Stationed in Guadalajara, he infiltrated the operations of the Guadalajara Cartel, led by Miguel Angel Felix Gallardo, Rafael Caro Quintero, and Ernesto Fonseca Carrillo. Camarena's intelligence led to the discovery and destruction of a massive marijuana plantation at Rancho Bufalo, reportedly worth billions of dollars, which made him a prime target for the cartel.

## Intelligence Connections

* According to the Amazon documentary series *The Last Narc* (2020), former DEA agents alleged that a CIA operative named Felix Ismael Rodriguez was present during Camarena's torture
* Camarena reportedly discovered that the CIA was working with the Guadalajara Cartel to funnel drug profits to fund the anti-communist Contras in Nicaragua
* Former DEA agent Hector Berrellez, who led the investigation (Operation Leyenda), alleged that CIA assets were directly involved in the kidnapping and interrogation
* The U.S. government reportedly investigated claims that a CIA operative and a DEA official's betrayal led to Camarena's murder
* The CIA has denied any involvement in or foreknowledge of Camarena's death

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Camarena's discovery of CIA-cartel-Contra connections gave the agency a motive to silence him
- Multiple former DEA agents have publicly stated they believe the CIA was complicit
- Audio tapes of the interrogation reportedly exist, and former agents claim a CIA operative's voice can be heard
- The Mexican police officers who kidnapped Camarena were allegedly on CIA payroll as assets
- The U.S. government initially resisted a full investigation into intelligence connections
- Rafael Caro Quintero was released from a Mexican prison in 2013 on a legal technicality before being recaptured in 2022

## Aftermath

The murder of Camarena triggered a massive U.S. response known as Operation Leyenda, one of the largest DEA homicide investigations in history. The investigation led to the arrest and conviction of several cartel figures, including Caro Quintero and Ernesto Fonseca Carrillo. The Mexican doctor who kept Camarena alive during torture, Humberto Alvarez Machain, was abducted from Mexico by DEA agents and brought to the U.S. for trial, creating a diplomatic crisis. In 2024, Camarena's family filed a civil lawsuit against the Sinaloa Cartel. The case remains a defining moment in DEA history, and Red Ribbon Week — the nation's largest drug prevention campaign — was founded in Camarena's honor.
