## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Frank Olson was a CIA biological weapons researcher who was secretly dosed with LSD by his colleague Sidney Gottlieb as part of the MKULTRA mind-control program. Nine days later, he fell from a 13th-floor hotel window. The CIA covered up its role for over 20 years. When Olson's body was exhumed in 1994, forensic experts found a cranial hematoma above the left eye inconsistent with a fall — suggesting he was struck before going through the window. Professor James Starrs, who led the forensic examination, called the evidence "rankly and starkly suggestive of homicide."

## Circumstances of Death

On November 18, 1953, a group of ten CIA and Army scientists gathered at a cabin in the forests of rural Maryland. During the retreat, CIA Technical Services Staff chief Sidney Gottlieb secretly spiked the after-dinner Cointreau with LSD. The participants had allegedly agreed earlier that "an unwitting experiment would be desirable," but Olson reportedly did not consent.

Olson's reaction to the LSD was severe. Over the following days, he exhibited symptoms described as paranoia and schizophrenia. He told his superiors he wanted to leave the biological weapons program. A few days before his death, he resigned as acting chief of the Special Operations Division at Fort Detrick, Maryland, citing a "severe moral crisis" about the nature of his biological weapons research.

CIA officials arranged for Olson to see a New York allergist, Dr. Harold Abramson, who was also connected to MKULTRA. On the night of November 27-28, Olson was staying in Room 1018A at the Hotel Statler with CIA officer Robert Lashbrook. According to Lashbrook, he woke at approximately 2:30 AM to find Olson had crashed through the closed window shade and glass, plunging 170 feet to the sidewalk below. Olson died on impact.

Hotel night manager Armand Pastore, who was the first to reach Olson's body, later stated that he found it suspicious that Lashbrook immediately made a phone call saying only "Well, he's gone" and then hung up, rather than calling for medical help.

## Background

Frank Olson was a bacteriologist and biological warfare scientist employed at the U.S. Army Biological Warfare Laboratories at Fort Detrick in Frederick, Maryland. He held a top-secret clearance and worked on some of the most classified programs in the American biological weapons arsenal.

Olson had reportedly traveled to Europe in the summer of 1953, where he allegedly observed extreme interrogation techniques at a CIA safe house in West Germany. Some researchers believe he witnessed or learned of interrogation methods involving biological agents that deeply disturbed him, contributing to his desire to leave the program.

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Olson was unwittingly drugged with LSD by a CIA colleague — an act that is itself a crime
- The only witness (CIA officer Lashbrook) gave an account that forensic evidence contradicts
- The 1994 exhumation revealed a large **hematoma above the left eye** that forensic experts said was inconsistent with the fall and likely occurred before the fall
- There was **no indication Frank ever went through glass** — contradicting the claim he crashed through a closed window
- The impact injuries on his chest were inconsistent with a simple fall
- Forensic expert James Starrs concluded Olson was likely struck unconscious and thrown from the window
- CIA Director Helms ordered the destruction of MKULTRA files in 1973
- Olson had expressed a desire to leave the program and was experiencing a "moral crisis" — making him a potential security risk to the CIA
- A CIA assassination manual from 1953, declassified years later, described defenestration (throwing someone from a high window) as an effective assassination technique
