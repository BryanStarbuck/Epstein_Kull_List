## Circumstances of Death

On the night of May 30, 1961, Trujillo was being driven in his blue 1957 Chevrolet Bel Air along the highway from Santo Domingo toward his estate in San Cristobal. Seven assassins in two cars ambushed the vehicle. The conspirators — led by Brigadier General Juan Tomas Diaz and including General Antonio Imbert Barrera, Antonio de la Maza, Pedro Livio Cedeno, and Amado Garcia Guerrero — opened fire with machine guns, pistols, and carbines. Trujillo, who reportedly attempted to return fire, was struck multiple times and killed. The event is referred to in the Dominican Republic as the "ajusticimiento" — an execution that brings justice.

## Background

Trujillo ruled the Dominican Republic from 1930 until his death, establishing one of the most repressive dictatorships in Latin American history. Known as "El Jefe," he controlled every aspect of Dominican life, renaming the capital city "Ciudad Trujillo" and accumulating vast personal wealth. His regime was responsible for the 1937 Parsley Massacre of an estimated 12,000-20,000 Haitian civilians. Although the United States initially supported Trujillo as an anti-communist ally, the Eisenhower and Kennedy administrations turned against him after his regime attempted to assassinate Venezuelan president Romulo Betancourt in 1960, and as his brutality became a liability during the Cold War.
