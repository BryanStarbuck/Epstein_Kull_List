## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Héctor Gutiérrez Ruiz was a prominent Uruguayan legislator murdered in exile as part of Operation Condor. His body was found alongside [Zelmar Michelini](Zelmar_Michelini.md) and two other victims in an abandoned car in Buenos Aires. Former Uruguayan dictator Juan María Bordaberry and Foreign Minister Juan Carlos Blanco were charged with orchestrating the murders in 2006.

## Circumstances of Death

On May 18, 1976, Gutiérrez Ruiz was abducted from his Buenos Aires residence by a paramilitary group. He was taken to a clandestine detention site where he was tortured. On May 21, his body was discovered in an abandoned Torino sedan at the corner of Perito Moreno and Dellepiane in Buenos Aires. Three other bodies were found in the same car: Senator Zelmar Michelini and two Tupamaros militants, William Whitelaw and Rosario del Carmen Barredo. All four victims had been tortured before being executed by gunshot.

## Background

Gutiérrez Ruiz was a member of the National Party (Partido Nacional) and served as President of the Chamber of Deputies — the most senior legislative position in Uruguay's lower house. He held this position at the time of the June 1973 coup d'état, after which he went into exile in Buenos Aires. In November 1975, the Uruguayan Embassy in Buenos Aires revoked the passports of Michelini, Gutiérrez Ruiz, and fellow exile Wilson Ferreira Aldunate, making it extremely difficult for them to leave Argentina. This deliberate act of transnational persecution trapped the exiles in a country that was itself about to fall to military dictatorship.
