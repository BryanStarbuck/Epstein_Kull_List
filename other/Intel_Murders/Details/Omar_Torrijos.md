## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Torrijos died in a plane crash just two years after signing the Panama Canal Treaties that transferred control of the canal from the United States to Panama. Multiple former intelligence operatives and insiders, most notably John Perkins in *Confessions of an Economic Hit Man*, have alleged CIA involvement. The official investigation was inconclusive, and key documents reportedly went missing during the 1989 U.S. invasion of Panama.

## Circumstances of Death

On July 31, 1981, Torrijos boarded a Panamanian Air Force Twin Otter (FAP-205) for a flight to Coclesito. The aircraft crashed into Cerro Marta hill in bad weather. All seven people on board were killed. A 1983 official investigation by Panamanian authorities, assisted by the FBI, attributed the crash to pilot error, citing disorientation, failure to maintain altitude relative to terrain, and low visibility. The investigation reportedly found no evidence of mechanical failure, explosives, or sabotage. However, the case was ultimately declared unsolved due to lack of evidence.

## Background

Torrijos was the sixth of twelve children born to two schoolteachers. He joined the Panamanian National Guard in 1952 and rose through the ranks, seizing power in a 1968 coup. Although never officially president, he held the title "Maximum Leader of the Panamanian Revolution" and was the de facto ruler of Panama from 1968 until his death. He is best known for negotiating the 1977 Torrijos-Carter Treaties with U.S. President Jimmy Carter, which guaranteed Panama would gain full sovereignty over the Panama Canal by 1999. At the time of his death, he was reportedly in negotiations with Japanese businessmen led by Shigeo Nagano over plans for a new, larger sea-level canal.
