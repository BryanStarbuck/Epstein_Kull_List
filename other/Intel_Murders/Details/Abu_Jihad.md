## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Israel denied responsibility for nearly 25 years but officially acknowledged the operation in November 2012 when censorship restrictions were lifted. The assassination, codenamed "Operation Introductory Lesson," was approved by a vote of 6-4 in Prime Minister Yitzhak Shamir's security cabinet. Israeli soldier Nahum Lev later publicly described killing Abu Jihad. This was one of the most elaborate Israeli extraterritorial operations of the Cold War era, involving naval commandos, Mossad agents, and air cover operating over 1,500 miles from Israel.

## Circumstances of Death

In the early morning hours of 16 April 1988, a flotilla of Israeli naval vessels approached the Tunisian coast. Twenty-six Israeli commandos reached shore by rubber boats, where Mossad agents who had been operating undercover in Tunisia drove them to Abu Jihad's villa in the Sidi Bou Said suburb of Tunis. One commando reportedly dressed as a woman to avoid suspicion during the approach. The team entered the villa, and when Abu Jihad reached for a weapon upon encountering the intruders, commando Nahum Lev shot and killed him. Abu Jihad was reportedly shot over 70 times. His wife, Intissar al-Wazir, and their children were present in the home but were not harmed. A bodyguard and a gardener were also killed. The entire operation lasted minutes, and the commandos withdrew to their naval vessels and departed Tunisian waters before authorities could respond.

## Background

Khalil Ibrahim al-Wazir, universally known by his nom de guerre Abu Jihad, was born in Ramla, Palestine in 1935. He became a refugee during the 1948 Arab-Israeli War, settling in Gaza. He co-founded the Fatah movement with Yasser Arafat in the late 1950s and became the PLO's top military commander and Arafat's closest deputy. Abu Jihad was responsible for planning and coordinating Palestinian guerrilla operations against Israel for decades. At the time of his killing, he was reportedly coordinating the First Intifada uprising in the occupied territories from Tunis, which made him a priority target for Israel's security establishment.

## Intelligence Connections

* The Israeli security cabinet under Prime Minister Yitzhak Shamir formally approved the assassination on 14 March 1988 by a vote of 6-4
* Mossad had planned the operation for over a year, with agents operating undercover in Tunisia conducting surveillance
* The operation involved coordination between Mossad, the Israeli Navy, Sayeret Matkal special forces, and the Israeli Air Force
* Ehud Barak, later Prime Minister, reportedly commanded the operation from a command ship offshore
* Israel denied responsibility until 2012, when former soldier Nahum Lev's account was published with government approval

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- The operation was carried out on the sovereign territory of Tunisia, over 1,500 miles from Israel, raising serious questions about international law
- Tunisia's government filed a formal protest at the United Nations Security Council
- Abu Jihad's wife and children were in the home during the killing
- The 25-year denial followed by acknowledgment raised questions about what other operations remain classified
- The assassination did not end Palestinian resistance — it arguably intensified the First Intifada
