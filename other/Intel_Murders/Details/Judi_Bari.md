# Judi Bari
Earth First! organizer who survived a car bombing in Oakland in 1990, was falsely accused by the FBI of carrying her own bomb, and later died of cancer in 1997. A federal jury awarded $4.4 million after finding FBI and Oakland Police violated her civil rights.

## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

While Bari died of cancer rather than the 1990 bombing, the FBI's conduct — falsely accusing her of carrying her own bomb, infiltrating Earth First!, and conducting what a federal jury determined was a COINTELPRO-style operation — represents a confirmed intelligence service assault on an American activist. A 2002 federal jury found that six of seven FBI and Oakland Police defendants violated Bari and co-activist Darryl Cherney's First and Fourth Amendment rights, awarding $4.4 million in damages.

## Circumstances of Death

Bari died of breast cancer on March 2, 1997, at her home in Willits, California. She was 47 years old. While her death from cancer is not itself suspicious, it came before her landmark civil rights lawsuit against the FBI reached trial. Bari had been organizing the legal case from her sickbed, and co-plaintiff Darryl Cherney carried the suit forward after her death.

The 1990 car bombing that nearly killed her remains officially unsolved. On May 24, 1990, a pipe bomb hidden directly under the driver's seat of her Subaru station wagon exploded as she drove through Oakland, California. Bari suffered a shattered pelvis and permanent injuries. Passenger Darryl Cherney sustained minor injuries.

## Background

Judi Bari was a labor organizer and environmental activist who became the leading voice of Earth First! in Northern California during the late 1980s. She organized "Redwood Summer" — a planned summer-long campaign of nonviolent direct action to stop the liquidation logging of old-growth redwood forests by Pacific Lumber Company, which had been taken over by corporate raider Charles Hurwitz through Maxxam Inc.

Bari was notable for steering Earth First! away from tree-spiking and toward nonviolent tactics modeled on the civil rights movement and labor organizing. She had received numerous death threats before the bombing, and timber industry publications had published her home address and vehicle description.

## Intelligence Connections

* The FBI had been conducting surveillance and infiltration of Earth First! prior to the bombing
* FBI bomb school at the Louisiana-Pacific mill site in Eureka, California, reportedly practiced on a car similar to Bari's just weeks before the bombing
* The FBI and Oakland Police arrested Bari and Cherney within hours of the bombing, claiming the bomb was theirs — despite the bomb being hidden under the driver's seat
* A federal jury found in 2002 that six of seven FBI and Oakland Police defendants had violated Bari's civil rights in a COINTELPRO-style operation
* FBI agent Frank Doyle, who led the bomb investigation, had conducted the bomb school training weeks earlier
* The actual bomber was never identified or prosecuted

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- The FBI falsely accused the victim of carrying her own bomb, despite forensic evidence showing it was hidden under her seat
- FBI bomb training exercises occurred near the bombing location weeks before the attack
- Earth First! was a documented target of FBI infiltration and disruption
- The bombing occurred on the eve of Redwood Summer, the campaign that most threatened timber industry interests
- The actual bomber was never caught, suggesting a possible cover-up
- A federal jury found the FBI and Oakland Police had framed Bari and Cherney
- Bari received death threats that were never seriously investigated by law enforcement
