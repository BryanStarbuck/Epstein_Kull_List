## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

The coroner explicitly stated: "I am not saying Mr Berezovsky took his own life, I am not saying Mr Berezovsky was unlawfully killed." The open verdict reflected the impossibility of determining the manner of death. BuzzFeed News reported that US intelligence agencies suspected 14 deaths of Russians in Britain were linked to Russian assassins — Berezovsky's was among them. His close friend Alexander Litvinenko had already been assassinated by Russian agents in London, and another associate Nikolai Glushkov was later murdered in 2018.

## Circumstances of Death

On March 23, 2013, Berezovsky's bodyguard found him dead in a locked bathroom at his home in Ascot, Berkshire. A ligature — reportedly a scarf — was around his neck, with the other end attached to a shower rail. The bathroom door was locked from the inside.

Specialists in chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear materials were deployed to the home as a precaution, given the precedent of Litvinenko's polonium poisoning. They reportedly found "nothing of concern."

A post-mortem examination found the cause of death was "consistent with hanging" and reported no indication of a violent struggle. However, at the inquest in March 2014, pathologist Professor Bernd Brinkmann from the University of Munster testified that the evidence was also consistent with strangulation by a third party.

## Background

Boris Berezovsky was one of Russia's original oligarchs, amassing enormous wealth during the 1990s privatization era. He controlled major media outlets, had significant interests in the Russian auto industry and Aeroflot airline, and wielded extraordinary political influence — reportedly playing a key role in Vladimir Putin's rise to the presidency.

After Putin consolidated power, Berezovsky fell out with the Kremlin and fled to the UK in 2000, where he was granted political asylum. From London, he became one of Putin's most vocal critics, funding opposition movements and calling for regime change in Russia.

In his final years, Berezovsky faced severe financial difficulties. He lost a massive lawsuit against Roman Abramovich in 2012 and was reportedly ordered to pay tens of millions in legal costs. He went through what was described as Britain's largest divorce settlement. Associates reported he was depressed and in financial distress.
