## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

While courts established that the Red Brigades carried out the kidnapping and murder, persistent and credible allegations suggest CIA and Gladio involvement. Moro was kidnapped on the exact day he was to sign an unprecedented accord bringing the Italian Communist Party into government -- an outcome the CIA had spent decades and billions of dollars preventing. A Gladio/SISMI officer was present near the ambush site. The Red Brigades destroyed all material referencing Gladio found during Moro's captivity. These circumstances have never been satisfactorily explained.

## Circumstances of Death

On March 16, 1978, Moro's motorcade was ambushed at 9:00 AM on Via Mario Fani in Rome by approximately 16 Red Brigades terrorists. The attackers killed all five of Moro's bodyguards in a precisely executed military-style assault and took Moro hostage.

For 55 days, Moro was held in a "people's prison" (a small apartment) while the Red Brigades conducted a "trial." During his captivity, Moro wrote numerous letters to political figures pleading for negotiations, but the Italian government maintained a firm "no negotiation" policy championed by his political rival Giulio Andreotti.

On May 9, 1978, Moro was shot 11 times in the back of a red Renault 4, which was then parked symbolically on Via Caetani, midway between the headquarters of the Christian Democrats and the Communist Party.

## Background

Aldo Moro was the president of Italy's Christian Democracy party and had served twice as Prime Minister. He was the architect of the "Historic Compromise" -- an unprecedented agreement to bring the Italian Communist Party (PCI) into a governing coalition with the Christian Democrats. This deal would have given the Communists an active role in Italian government for the first time since World War II.

Moro was kidnapped on the very morning the Historic Compromise was to be formalized in Parliament.

## Intelligence Connections

- Colonel Camillo Guglielmi of SISMI's 7th Division, which controlled Operation Gladio, was present on Via Stresa near the ambush location at the time of the attack. He claimed to have been invited for lunch -- but the ambush occurred at 9:00 AM
- The Red Brigades later stated they destroyed all material containing references to Operation Gladio discovered during Moro's captivity
- One theory holds that the decision to kill Moro was made during the fourth week of captivity, when he was believed to be revealing state secrets about Gladio in his letters
- The CIA had spent decades and vast sums preventing Communist participation in Italian government through Operation Gladio and other covert programs
- Former CIA officer Richard Brenneke claimed the CIA funded and supported the Red Brigades through Gladio assets
- The precision of the Via Fani ambush -- killing five bodyguards without hitting Moro -- suggested professional military training beyond what the Red Brigades were known to possess
