## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

While a local labourer named Andrew George was convicted of Hilda Murrell's murder in 2005 based on DNA and fingerprint evidence, the case remains deeply controversial. Her nephew was Commander Robert Green, a former Royal Navy intelligence officer privy to details of the sinking of the Argentine ship General Belgrano during the Falklands War. MP Tam Dalyell alleged that MI5 agents conducted a burglary of Murrell's home — possibly searching for Belgrano-related documents — and that the situation escalated into her murder. In 1983, the Ministry of Defence had established a specialist MI5 unit, Defence Secretariat 19, specifically to disrupt anti-nuclear campaigns.

## Circumstances of Death

On March 21, 1984, Hilda Murrell's home in Shrewsbury was broken into and a small amount of cash was taken. She was then abducted in her own car — a white Renault 5 — which multiple witnesses reported seeing driven erratically through town, even past the police station, during the lunch hour. The car was later found abandoned in a lane outside the town. It took West Mercia Police three days to find her body in a copse across a field from her car. She had been beaten and stabbed multiple times, but did not die from those injuries — she died of hypothermia, suggesting she was left alive in the field and froze to death over hours.

## Background

Hilda Murrell was a highly respected rose grower and naturalist who ran the nursery Edwin Murrell Ltd, winning awards at Chelsea, Southport, and Shrewsbury flower shows and reportedly selling roses to the Queen Mother. She was also a committed anti-nuclear campaigner. At the time of her death, she was preparing to present a paper titled "An Ordinary Citizen's View of Radioactive Waste Management" at the Sizewell B Inquiry — the first major public planning inquiry into a new British nuclear power station. Her nephew, Commander Robert Green RN (retired), had served as a naval intelligence officer during the Falklands War and was one of a handful of people privy to details of the sinking of the General Belgrano.

## Intelligence Connections

* In 1983, the Ministry of Defence established Defence Secretariat 19 (DS19), a specialist MI5 unit, reportedly to disrupt anti-nuclear disarmament campaigns and movements opposing nuclear energy expansion
* MP Tam Dalyell raised the case in Parliament and alleged that the secret services were involved in Murrell's death
* Murrell's family says one of Dalyell's sources was "a famous and experienced former MI6 officer"
* Her nephew Commander Robert Green suspects MI5 involvement and believes agents may have burgled the house looking for Belgrano-related documents
* In March 2012, Michael Mansfield QC called for an inquiry into what MI5 knew about the case
* Andrew George, convicted in 2005, was a 16-year-old local labourer at the time of the murder — some question whether a teenager could have carried out the abduction alone
