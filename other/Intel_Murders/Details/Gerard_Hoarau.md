# Gerard Hoarau
Seychellois opposition leader in exile, shot dead on his doorstep in London in 1985. The murder remains unsolved, with suspicion falling on the Seychelles government of France-Albert Rene and possible South African intelligence involvement.

## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Hoarau was gunned down in a professional assassination on his London doorstep. The killing bears hallmarks of a state-sponsored operation: the assassin had precise knowledge of Hoarau's schedule (obtained through phone surveillance), fired 11 shots, and escaped without a trace. British police have never identified the killer. Evidence points to the Rene government in Seychelles, which had been conducting surveillance on Hoarau from a London safe house, with possible South African intelligence involvement given the shifting geopolitics of the region.

## Circumstances of Death

On the evening of November 29, 1985, Hoarau returned to his home in Edgware, North London. An unidentified gunman was waiting on his doorstep and fired 11 times, striking Hoarau with three bullets. He died at the scene. The assassin and an accomplice fled and have never been identified. British Metropolitan Police investigated but the case remains unsolved. In 2018, a 77-year-old man was arrested in Antrim, Northern Ireland, in connection with the murder, but the case did not result in a conviction. In 2023, Met Police renewed their appeal for information.

## Background

Hoarau was born in Seychelles and became politically active in opposition to France-Albert Rene, who had seized power in a coup d'etat on June 5, 1977. Hoarau left Seychelles in 1979 and settled in London, where he founded and led the Mouvement Pour La Resistance (MPR), which sought the peaceful restoration of democracy in Seychelles. He became the most prominent voice of the Seychellois exile opposition. His activism made him a direct threat to the Rene regime, which had survived multiple coup attempts, including a 1981 attempt involving South African mercenaries.

## Intelligence Connections

* President Rene's government had Hoarau's London phone tapped from a safe house purchased with funds transferred from a secret account in Jersey
* Surveillance recordings captured details about Hoarau's schedule, including changes to a doctor's appointment — information the assassin reportedly used to time the attack
* South African intelligence operative Craig Williamson, known for letter-bomb assassinations of anti-apartheid activists, had established ties with Rene associate Mario Ricci
* Research by Martin Plaut, based on UK National Archive files, revealed "murky involvement of the South African government in the destabilization of the Seychelles in the 1980s"
* The relationship between South Africa and the Rene government shifted over time from hostility to cooperation, complicating the question of who ordered the assassination

- The assassination demonstrated professional tradecraft: precise timing, knowledge of the target's movements, clean escape

- The killer has never been identified despite decades of investigation by British police
- The Rene government was the party with the clearest motive to silence Hoarau
- South African intelligence was active in Seychelles politics during this period, and the geopolitical dynamics were complex
- A 2018 arrest in Northern Ireland suggests possible use of hired operatives from outside the Seychelles
- Despite renewed police appeals in 2023, the case remains unsolved nearly four decades later
- The Rene government survived until 2004, and Hoarau's killing effectively decapitated the exile opposition
- Hoarau had survived previous threats and was aware he was a target
