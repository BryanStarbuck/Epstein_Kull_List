## Assessment: CONFIRMED

The investigation confirmed a contract killing. Gunman Miroslav Marcek confessed and was sentenced. Accomplice Tomas Szabo was also convicted. Middleman Zoltan Andrusko cooperated with investigators. The case against alleged mastermind Marian Kocner has been through multiple trials, with Alena Zsuzsova convicted of commissioning the murder in 2023 (verdict later overturned for retrial). Slovak police confirmed Kuciak was murdered because of his work as an investigative journalist.

## Circumstances of Death

On the evening of February 21, 2018, gunman Miroslav Marcek entered the home of Jan Kuciak and his fiancee Martina Kusnirova in Velka Maca, a village in western Slovakia. Marcek shot Kuciak twice in the chest. Kusnirova, 27, was shot once in the head. Accomplice Tomas Szabo waited in a car nearby.

The bodies were discovered on February 25, four days after the murders.

## Background

Kuciak worked as an investigative reporter for the news website Aktuality.sk, one of Slovakia's most widely read online publications. He focused on investigating tax fraud and corruption, particularly the connections between Italian organized crime (the 'Ndrangheta) and top-level Slovak politicians, including those in the ruling Smer party of Prime Minister Robert Fico.

He was the first journalist murdered in Slovakia since the country's independence in 1993. Kusnirova was a 27-year-old archaeology student.

## Intelligence Connections

- While not a traditional intelligence service killing, the murder exposed deep connections between organized crime and the Slovak political establishment
- Alleged mastermind Marian Kocner was a well-connected Slovak businessman with ties to senior politicians
- Kuciak's reporting investigated how the Italian 'Ndrangheta had penetrated the Slovak state, with members holding positions in government agencies
- The murder revealed systemic corruption reaching into the highest levels of Slovak government

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- The double murder of a journalist and his innocent fiancee in their home was unprecedented in post-Communist Central Europe
- The murders triggered massive protests across Slovakia, the largest since the Velvet Revolution
- Prime Minister Robert Fico and his entire cabinet resigned on March 15, 2018
- Kuciak's last unfinished article, published posthumously, detailed Italian mafia activities in Slovakia and their ties to people close to PM Fico
- The legal proceedings have been complicated, with the alleged mastermind Kocner initially acquitted, then retried
- Kocner had threatened Kuciak before the murder and had extensive surveillance records on him
