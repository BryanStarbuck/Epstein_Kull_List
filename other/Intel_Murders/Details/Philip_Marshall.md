## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Philip Marshall, a former contract pilot with CIA connections who flew for notorious drug smuggler Barry Seal, was found dead alongside his two teenage children and the family dog — all shot in the head. The Calaveras County Sheriff ruled it a murder-suicide, but multiple anomalies at the scene, Marshall's history of exposing CIA operations, and the timing relative to a new book he was reportedly writing have led former intelligence professionals to call it a professional hit. Former NSA officer Wayne Madsen stated he was "100% certain" it was a black ops assassination.

## Circumstances of Death

On February 2, 2013, the bodies of Philip Marshall, his son Alex (17), and daughter Macaila (14) were discovered in their home in Murphys, California. All three had been shot in the head. The family dog was also shot dead. A handgun was found near Marshall's body. The Calaveras County Sheriff's Office ruled it a murder-suicide within days, concluding that Marshall shot his children on the couch, shot the dog, and then killed himself.

Neighbors reported hearing nothing unusual. There were no signs of forced entry. The bodies were reportedly not discovered for several days. Friends and neighbors described Marshall as a loving father with no history of violence or mental illness, and said the notion that he would execute his own children was inconceivable.

## Background

Marshall was a commercial airline pilot who, according to his own account and published works, had worked as a contract pilot for the CIA during the 1980s. He reportedly flew missions for Barry Seal, the notorious CIA-connected drug smuggler at the center of the Mena, Arkansas cocaine trafficking operation. Marshall maintained relationships with intelligence contacts throughout his life.

He authored three books: *Lakefront Airport* (2003), a novel based on his experiences flying with Seal; *False Flag 911: How Bush, Cheney and the Saudis Created the Post-911 World* (2008); and *The Big Bamboozle: 9/11 and the War on Terror* (2012). In *The Big Bamboozle*, Marshall argued that the 9/11 attacks were orchestrated by US and Saudi government officials, not al-Qaeda alone, and that the hijacker pilots were trained at a secret CIA airfield in Arizona. He was reportedly working on a fourth book at the time of his death that would name specific officials.

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Neighbors universally described Marshall as a devoted father incapable of harming his children
- No history of mental illness, domestic violence, or suicidal ideation
- The family dog was also shot — unusual in murder-suicides but consistent with eliminating a noise source during a professional hit
- Former NSA officer Wayne Madsen conducted a week-long investigation and stated he was "100% certain" it was a black ops hit
- Marshall was reportedly working on a new book that would name specific US and Saudi officials
- The Sheriff's investigation was concluded rapidly with minimal forensic analysis
- No suicide note was found
- Friends reported Marshall had expressed concern about his safety due to his writing
- The "murder-suicide" of an author, his children, and pet mirrors a pattern sometimes attributed to staged intelligence killings designed to discredit the victim
