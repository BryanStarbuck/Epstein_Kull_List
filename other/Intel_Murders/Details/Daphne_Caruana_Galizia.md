# Daphne Caruana Galizia
Maltese investigative journalist assassinated by car bomb near her home after exposing high-level corruption linked to the Panama Papers. A public inquiry found the Maltese state bore responsibility.

## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Multiple convictions have been secured. Brothers Alfred and George Degiorgio were sentenced to 40 years for installing and detonating the bomb. Vincent Muscat received 15 years after turning state's evidence. Robert Agius and Jamie Vella were found guilty of supplying the bomb. Businessman Yorgen Fenech was charged as the alleged mastermind. A 437-page public inquiry report found the Maltese state, including former PM Joseph Muscat and his cabinet, bore responsibility for creating an "atmosphere of impunity."

## Circumstances of Death

On October 16, 2017, at approximately 3:00 PM, Caruana Galizia left her home in Bidnija in her rented Peugeot 108. Shortly after, a powerful car bomb detonated inside the vehicle, killing her almost instantly. Debris from the car was scattered over a wide area. The bomb was reportedly a military-grade device triggered by remote detonation via an SMS.

Her son Matthew heard the explosion from their family home and ran to the scene.

## Background

Caruana Galizia was Malta's most prominent investigative journalist, running a widely-read blog called "Running Commentary" that attracted more readers than Malta's newspapers combined. She was a one-woman investigative powerhouse who exposed corruption at the highest levels of Maltese politics and business.

Her most significant reporting came in connection with the Panama Papers, which revealed that Maltese politicians -- including then-PM Joseph Muscat's chief of staff Keith Schembri and energy minister Konrad Mizzi -- held secret offshore accounts. She also uncovered connections between Maltese officials and the Dubai-based company 17 Black, owned by Yorgen Fenech, a wealthy Maltese businessman with interests in energy.

In the weeks before her death, Caruana Galizia wrote on her blog: "There are crooks everywhere you look now. The situation is desperate."

## Intelligence Connections

- While not a traditional intelligence service killing, the assassination involved organized crime figures with connections to Maltese political power
- The public inquiry found that the state created an "atmosphere of impunity" that emboldened the killers
- The bomb was a sophisticated military-grade device, suggesting access to specialized materials
- Yorgen Fenech, the alleged mastermind, had close ties to the highest levels of the Maltese government
- Keith Schembri, the PM's chief of staff, was also implicated in corruption Caruana Galizia exposed

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- A public inquiry by former judges found the Maltese state -- including PM Joseph Muscat and his entire cabinet -- bore responsibility for creating conditions that enabled the murder
- The investigation was initially stalled until middleman Melvin Theuma came forward after being granted a presidential pardon
- Former PM Muscat resigned in December 2019 amid the political crisis triggered by the investigation
- Caruana Galizia had filed 48 complaints with police about threats and harassment in the 14 months before her death -- none were acted upon
- Her murder triggered an EU-wide reckoning about press freedom and rule of law in Malta
- The European Parliament held multiple debates about delayed justice in the case
