## Assessment: CONFIRMED (assassination plot)

The Ukrainian Security Service (SBU) confirmed that Russian intelligence services had recruited a Ukrainian citizen to organize the assassination of Arkady Babchenko. The operation to fake his death, while controversial, reportedly led to the arrest of the person who had been paid $40,000 to arrange the hit. The existence of a genuine Russian plot to kill Babchenko has not been seriously disputed.

## The Faked Assassination

On the evening of 29 May 2018, Ukrainian police reported that Babchenko had been found shot in his Kyiv apartment, apparently killed by multiple gunshot wounds. His wife reportedly found him bleeding on the floor. Photographs circulated showing Babchenko lying face-down in a pool of blood. World leaders, press freedom organizations, and journalists condemned the killing and blamed Russian intelligence.

Approximately 24 hours later, on 30 May 2018, Babchenko walked into a press conference held by the SBU, very much alive. SBU chief Vasily Gritsak revealed that the "assassination" had been staged using pig blood and cooperation from Babchenko's wife. The operation had been planned for two months.

## Background

Babchenko served as a conscript in the Russian army during both Chechen wars and became one of Russia's most prominent war correspondents. He wrote extensively about the brutality of the Chechen conflicts and became an outspoken critic of the Kremlin's military operations and political repression.

After years of increasing threats, Babchenko left Russia in February 2017, moving first to Prague, then to Israel, and finally to Kyiv. His criticism of the Kremlin — including his refusal to mourn the crash of a Russian military plane carrying the Alexandrov Ensemble choir in 2016 — generated intense hostility from Russian nationalists and, reportedly, from Russian intelligence services.

## Intelligence Connections

* The SBU stated that Russian special services recruited a Ukrainian citizen, identified as Boris German, who was tasked with finding perpetrators for multiple "high-impact terrorist acts," including Babchenko's assassination
* German reportedly paid $40,000 for the contract on Babchenko and was arrested during the operation
* The SBU claimed the plot was part of a broader Russian intelligence operation targeting multiple individuals in Ukraine
* Babchenko cooperated with Ukrainian intelligence (SBU) to stage the operation
* The case demonstrated the active targeting of exiled Russian journalists by Russian intelligence services

## Why This Case Matters

- Confirmed that Russian intelligence was actively contracting assassinations of exiled journalists in Ukraine
- The recruited intermediary, Boris German, was arrested and reportedly confessed to organizing the hit on behalf of Russian intelligence
- Babchenko's case fits a documented pattern: Russian journalists and critics in exile being targeted by FSB/GRU operations
- The faked death operation, while successful in its immediate goal, was widely criticized for undermining media credibility and public trust
- Demonstrates the operational reach of Russian intelligence in targeting critics abroad — even in a country actively hostile to Russia
