## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

While five Chechen men were convicted of carrying out the murder, the investigation never identified who ordered the assassination. A 2022 Bellingcat investigation revealed that FSB agents from the Second Service had been tailing Nemtsov for ten months prior to his murder — and stopped surveilling him just one trip before the killing. The Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe called on Russia to reopen the investigation, citing "serious concerns over its independence and effectiveness." The murder of Russia's most prominent opposition figure, within sight of the Kremlin's walls and within range of its security cameras, raises fundamental questions about state complicity.

## Circumstances of Death

On the evening of February 27, 2015, Boris Nemtsov was walking home across the Bolshoy Moskvoretsky Bridge in central Moscow after dinner at a GUM restaurant with his Ukrainian girlfriend, Anna Duritskaya, 23. At approximately 11:31 PM, an unknown assailant fired seven or eight shots from a Makarov pistol. Four bullets struck Nemtsov — in the head, heart, liver, and stomach — killing him almost instantly.

The killer had been waiting in the vicinity and fled the scene immediately. Duritskaya, the sole eyewitness, survived the attack unharmed.

The murder took place on one of the most heavily surveilled locations in Moscow, directly adjacent to the Kremlin. However, according to reports, the Kremlin security cameras covering the bridge were conveniently "under maintenance" at the time of the shooting. A snow-clearing truck partially obscured views from other cameras.

Nemtsov was shot just two days before he had planned to lead a major opposition rally against Russia's war in Ukraine, and he had been preparing a report documenting evidence of Russian military involvement in eastern Ukraine.

## Background

Boris Nemtsov was one of Russia's most prominent liberal politicians and opposition leaders. In the 1990s, he served as Governor of Nizhny Novgorod and was widely considered President Yeltsin's likely successor. He served as First Deputy Prime Minister from 1997 to 1998.

After Putin rose to power, Nemtsov became one of the Kremlin's most outspoken critics. He co-founded several opposition movements and regularly organized protests against corruption and authoritarianism. He served in the Duma and was a co-chair of the Republican Party of Russia-People's Freedom Party.

In his final years, Nemtsov was especially vocal about:
- Russia's military intervention in Ukraine (he was preparing a report titled "Putin. War.")
- Calling on Western nations to impose sanctions on Putin's associates
- Corruption within the Putin government
- The deterioration of civil liberties in Russia

## Intelligence Connections

- **FSB surveillance:** Bellingcat revealed in March 2022 that FSB agents Valery Sukharev, Dmitry Sukhinin, and Aleksei Krivoshchyokov had been tailing Nemtsov for 10 months. They began surveillance after Nemtsov publicly called for Western sanctions against Putin's associates. The surveillance stopped just one trip before the assassination.
- **Chechen security forces:** The convicted gunman, Zaur Dadayev, was a former deputy commander of the Chechen "Sever" Battalion, which operates under the Russian Interior Ministry. He was reportedly close to Chechen leader Ramzan Kadyrov.
- **Mastermind never identified:** The trial established that Ruslan Geremeyev, a senior Chechen military officer and relative of a member of the Russian Federation Council, directed the assassins. Geremeyev was never arrested, and reportedly fled to the UAE.
- **Kremlin cameras:** The most surveilled bridge in Moscow had its cameras "under maintenance" during the killing
- **Russia refused** to investigate the chain of command above the convicted gunmen

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Nemtsov was killed on a bridge adjacent to the Kremlin, one of the most heavily guarded locations in Moscow
- Kremlin-operated security cameras were reportedly not functioning at the time
- FSB had been surveilling Nemtsov for months and stopped just before the murder
- The convicted killers were Chechen security forces with ties to Kadyrov, a Putin loyalist
- The investigation never pursued who ordered the killing
- Senior Chechen military officer Ruslan Geremeyev, identified as the organizer, was never arrested
- Nemtsov was killed days before a planned anti-war rally and while preparing evidence of Russian military intervention in Ukraine
- The Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe found the investigation inadequate
- Neither the official investigation nor the trial established a motive
