## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Anastasia Baburova became the fourth Novaya Gazeta journalist killed since 2000. While her murder was attributed to neo-Nazi militants, the killing occurred in the context of systematic violence against journalists in Putin's Russia. Baburova was investigating the very neo-Nazi networks that killed her, and the broader question of state tolerance for militant nationalist violence remains unresolved.

## Circumstances of Death

On 19 January 2009, Baburova accompanied human rights lawyer Stanislav Markelov to a press conference at the Independent Press Center in Moscow. She was working on a story for Novaya Gazeta about anti-fascist movements and human rights. As they walked together after the press conference, Nikita Tikhonov shot Markelov in the head with a silenced pistol.

According to witnesses, Baburova attempted to chase the attacker. Tikhonov turned and shot her in the back of the head. She was taken to a Moscow hospital but died several hours later from her wounds. She was 25 years old and in her fifth year of journalism studies at Moscow State University.

## Background

Baburova was a journalism student at Moscow State University and a freelance journalist for Novaya Gazeta, Russia's most independent newspaper. She was a member of Autonomous Action, an anarchist organization, and had been investigating Russian neo-Nazi groups for Novaya Gazeta since October 2008. Previously, she had worked on the editorial team of the newspaper Izvestia throughout 2008.

Her work for Novaya Gazeta placed her in a long and tragic lineage. The newspaper had already lost three journalists to murder since 2000, most notably Anna Politkovskaya, who was shot dead in her apartment building in October 2006.

## Intelligence Connections

* Baburova was investigating neo-Nazi groups that reportedly operated with a degree of state tolerance in Russia
* Novaya Gazeta, her employer, had been a persistent target of the Russian security establishment — four of its journalists were killed between 2000 and 2009
* The BORN organization that carried out the killing has been linked by analysts to broader networks of nationalist violence that some allege the FSB tolerated or manipulated
* Her death occurred in a climate where journalism critical of state power had become lethally dangerous in Russia

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Fourth Novaya Gazeta journalist killed since 2000 — a pattern suggesting systematic targeting
- Was actively investigating the neo-Nazi networks that killed her
- Shot in broad daylight in central Moscow with apparent impunity
- The ECHR later ruled that the trial of her killers was unfair, raising questions about the integrity of the investigation
- Convicted killer Yevgenia Khasis was reportedly released from prison in 2025, having served her sentence, in a Russia reportedly "more aligned with her worldview than ever" according to Novaya Gazeta Europe
