## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

The UN Commission of Inquiry found that Pakistani officials, including ISI, "failed profoundly" in their duty to protect Bhutto and played a key role in the subsequent cover-up. The crime scene was hosed down within hours, destroying critical evidence. Police were intimidated by intelligence agencies from conducting a proper investigation. While the UN Commission did not conclusively identify who ordered the assassination, the ISI's involvement in both the security failures and the cover-up points to intelligence complicity.

## Circumstances of Death

On December 27, 2007, Bhutto attended a political rally at Liaqat National Bagh park in Rawalpindi. As she was leaving in her armored vehicle, she stood up through the sunroof to wave to supporters. A gunman fired shots at her, and moments later a suicide bomber detonated an explosive vest nearby, killing 23 other people.

The Pakistani government initially claimed Bhutto died from hitting her head on the sunroof lever, not from bullet wounds -- a claim that was widely disputed. The crime scene was controversially hosed down within hours of the attack, destroying forensic evidence.

## Background

Benazir Bhutto was the first woman to serve as Prime Minister of a Muslim-majority country, serving two terms (1988-1990 and 1993-1996). She was the daughter of former PM Zulfikar Ali Bhutto, who was executed by General Zia ul-Haq's military regime in 1979. She had returned to Pakistan on October 18, 2007, after eight years of self-imposed exile, to campaign in upcoming elections.

Her return was itself marked by violence: a bombing at her welcome rally in Karachi on October 18 killed 139 people.

## Intelligence Connections

- ISI Director General warned Bhutto on the morning of her assassination about specific threats
- Despite these warnings, the government failed to provide adequate security
- The UN investigation found ISI played a key role in the cover-up, intimidating police from doing their jobs
- The British newspaper The Times suggested elements within ISI with ties to Islamists may have been behind the killing
- Musharraf's government had a clear political motive: Bhutto's return threatened his hold on power
- The Taliban (Baitullah Mehsud faction) claimed responsibility, but questions remain about ISI connections to these groups
- Pakistan's intelligence agencies have long-documented connections to jihadist groups

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- The crime scene was hosed down within hours, destroying evidence -- an act the UN found deliberate
- ISI officers intimidated local police from conducting a proper investigation
- The government's initial false claim about cause of death (sunroof lever) suggested a cover-up
- Despite ISI having specific intelligence about threats, security was inadequate
- Bhutto herself had written a letter naming those she believed would try to kill her, reportedly including senior Pakistani officials
- The UN Commission found the investigation was "severely hampered" from the outset
- No one has been definitively convicted as the organizer of the attack
