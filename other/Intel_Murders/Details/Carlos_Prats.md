# Carlos Prats

## Assessment: CONFIRMED

The assassination of Carlos Prats and his wife Sofia Cuthbert was carried out by DINA, Chile's secret police, on the direct orders of General Augusto Pinochet. DINA director Manuel Contreras and operative Michael Townley were convicted for the murders. Declassified CIA documents confirm U.S. intelligence awareness of the operation. This was the first assassination carried out by the Pinochet regime outside Chile and a precursor to the broader Operation Condor campaign of transnational political killings.

## Circumstances of Death

On September 30, 1974, shortly after midnight, General Prats and his wife Sofia returned to their apartment building in the Palermo neighborhood of Buenos Aires after visiting friends. As Prats drove their small Fiat 125 into the parking garage, a radio-controlled bomb attached to the underside of the car detonated, killing both of them instantly. DINA operative Michael Townley, accompanied by his wife Mariana Callejas (also a DINA agent), had planted the explosive device on the car's chassis the previous day, September 29. Townley and Callejas staked out the building until the Prats returned. According to accounts, Callejas initially attempted to trigger the detonation, but the device failed; it subsequently detonated when the car was moved.

## Background

Prats was Pinochet's predecessor as Commander-in-Chief of the Chilean Army and had served as Minister of the Interior and Defense under President Salvador Allende. He was a constitutionalist who supported civilian democratic government and refused to participate in military plots against Allende. He resigned his command in August 1973 under pressure from right-wing officers and was replaced by Pinochet, who then led the September 11, 1973 coup. After the coup, Prats went into exile in Buenos Aires, where he was writing his memoirs. Pinochet considered Prats a threat because of his stature within the military and his potential to rally opposition to the junta.

## Intelligence Connections

* Pinochet personally ordered DINA director Manuel Contreras to eliminate Prats in a meeting in June 1974, according to declassified documents from the National Security Archive
* Contreras assigned DINA station chief in Argentina, Enrique Arancibia Clavel, to coordinate the operation with Argentine paramilitary groups
* DINA operative Michael Townley built and planted the car bomb
* Arancibia Clavel was later convicted in Argentine courts for his role
* The operation was a precursor to Operation Condor, the multinational intelligence coordination between Southern Cone dictatorships to track and eliminate political opponents across borders
* CIA weekly situation reports documented the assassination, indicating U.S. intelligence was monitoring the situation

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Prats was assassinated in a foreign country (Argentina), establishing the pattern of extraterritorial assassination that would define Operation Condor
- His wife Sofia Cuthbert, an Argentine citizen with no political role, was also killed — a civilian casualty
- The same DINA operative (Michael Townley) went on to assassinate Orlando Letelier in Washington, DC, two years later
- Pinochet's direct order to assassinate his predecessor demonstrates the regime's willingness to kill even senior military figures
- The operation required cooperation from Argentine paramilitary networks, revealing the transnational infrastructure of state terror
- It took decades for convictions to be obtained
