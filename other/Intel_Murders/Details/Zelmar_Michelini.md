## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Zelmar Michelini was a sitting Uruguayan senator murdered on his 52nd birthday in Buenos Aires as part of Operation Condor — the US-backed coordination of South American dictatorships to eliminate political opponents across borders. Former Uruguayan dictator Juan María Bordaberry and Foreign Minister Juan Carlos Blanco were charged with orchestrating the murders in 2006. Additional military officers were convicted in 2022.

## Circumstances of Death

At 4:00 AM on May 18, 1976, heavily armed agents in civilian clothing abducted Michelini from the Liberty Hotel in Buenos Aires. He was taken to a clandestine detention site and tortured. On May 21, his body was found in an abandoned Torino sedan at the corner of Perito Moreno and Dellepiane in Buenos Aires. Three other bodies were in the same car: [Héctor Gutiérrez Ruiz](Hector_Gutierrez_Ruiz.md), former Speaker of the Uruguayan Chamber of Deputies, and two Tupamaros militants, William Whitelaw and Rosario del Carmen Barredo. All four had been tortured before being executed.

## Background

Michelini served in the Uruguayan Chamber of Deputies from 1954 to 1958 and the Chamber of Senators from 1966. A centrist politician affiliated with the Colorado Party and later the Frente Amplio coalition, he was a vocal critic of Uruguay's slide toward dictatorship. After the June 1973 Uruguayan coup d'état, he fled to Buenos Aires. The Uruguayan Embassy in Buenos Aires revoked his passport in November 1975, trapping him in Argentina and making it impossible for him to flee further — a deliberate act of transnational coordination by the Uruguayan dictatorship.

## Intelligence Connections

* The Uruguayan Embassy withdrew Michelini's passport in November 1975, demonstrating direct Uruguayan government involvement in targeting him

* Former Uruguayan dictator Juan María Bordaberry was charged in 2006 with ordering the murders
* In August 2022, Uruguayan military officers José Arab, Ernesto Ramas, Jorge Silveira, and Ricardo Medina were convicted for the killings
