## Assessment: CONFIRMED

This is one of the most thoroughly documented cases of a U.S. government agency coordinating the assassination of a domestic political figure. FBI COINTELPRO provided a detailed floor plan of Hampton's apartment — obtained from paid informant William O'Neal — to the Cook County State's Attorney's police tactical unit. O'Neal drugged Hampton's drink that evening. Police fired over 99 rounds into the apartment; forensic analysis determined only one shot came from the Panthers' side. Hampton was shot twice in the head at close range while unconscious in his bed. FBI Director J. Edgar Hoover sent a letter thanking the agent who coordinated the raid and authorized a $200 bonus for "outstanding services." The families received a $1.85 million settlement in 1982.

## Circumstances of Death

At approximately 4:30 AM on December 4, 1969, fourteen officers from the Cook County State's Attorney's Office tactical unit, armed with a Thompson submachine gun, shotguns, a .357 Magnum, handguns, and a .30-caliber M1 Carbine, raided Hampton's apartment at 2337 West Monroe Street on Chicago's West Side.

Police kicked down the front door and immediately began firing. They sprayed more than 99 gunshots throughout the apartment, including machine gun rounds through exterior walls and windows. Seven Black Panther members were inside, including Hampton, who was asleep in bed with his pregnant fiancee, Deborah Johnson (later Akua Njeri).

Ballistics experts later determined that only one of those bullets came from the Panthers' side — a single shotgun blast fired by Mark Clark as he was killed, likely a reflexive discharge. This contradicted police claims of a fierce gun battle.

Hampton was found shot twice in the head. According to survivors and forensic evidence, he was unconscious when killed — never able to respond or defend himself. Witnesses reported that after the initial barrage, two officers entered the bedroom and fired point-blank into Hampton's head.

FBI informant William O'Neal had slipped a barbiturate (secobarbital) into Hampton's Kool-Aid that evening, ensuring he would be deeply sedated during the raid. Toxicology confirmed the presence of barbiturates in Hampton's blood.

Mark Clark, a 22-year-old Panther leader from Peoria, Illinois, was also killed in the raid.

## Background

Fred Hampton grew up in Maywood, Illinois, a suburb of Chicago. He excelled academically and athletically in high school. He led the NAACP Youth Council, growing membership to over 500.

In 1968, at age 20, Hampton joined the Black Panther Party and became chairman of the Illinois chapter. He was a gifted orator and brilliant organizer. He founded the **Rainbow Coalition** — a multicultural political alliance uniting the Black Panthers, the Young Patriots (poor white Appalachian migrants), and the Young Lords (Puerto Rican activists). This coalition building across racial lines made Hampton especially threatening to the FBI.

Hampton organized free breakfast programs for children, free medical clinics, and political education classes. He negotiated gang truces and redirected gang members toward community organizing. FBI documents show the Bureau was alarmed by Hampton's ability to unite people across racial lines.

## Intelligence Connections

* **COINTELPRO:** The FBI's Counter Intelligence Program specifically targeted Hampton as a "key militant leader" and sought to prevent the rise of a "Black Messiah" who could unify the movement
* **William O'Neal:** The FBI's paid informant infiltrated the Illinois Panthers, became Hampton's personal bodyguard and head of security, provided the FBI with a detailed floor plan of Hampton's apartment, and drugged Hampton's drink on the night of the raid
* **FBI floor plan:** The FBI provided the apartment layout — including the exact location of Hampton's bed — to the Cook County State's Attorney's tactical unit, which executed the raid
* **J. Edgar Hoover's approval:** In a letter dated December 10, 1969 — six days after the raid — FBI Director J. Edgar Hoover thanked Roy Martin Mitchell, the Chicago FBI agent who coordinated the operation, for his "exemplary efforts" and approved a $200 incentive award for "outstanding services in a matter of considerable interest to the FBI in the racial field"
* **Forensic evidence of assassination:** A federal grand jury investigation found that of 82-99 shots fired, only one came from the Panthers. The physical evidence indicated a planned execution, not a gunfight
* **O'Neal's later fate:** William O'Neal committed suicide in 1990, reportedly haunted by his role in Hampton's death

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- The FBI provided the floor plan of Hampton's apartment, including the location of his bed, to the raiding officers
- Hampton was drugged by an FBI informant before the raid
- Police fired 99 rounds; only 1 shot came from the Panthers' side
- Hampton was shot twice in the head while unconscious in bed
- The FBI rewarded the coordinating agent with a bonus and commendation
- Evidence at the scene was tampered with — police initially claimed Panthers fired first
- A subsequent investigation by a commission of inquiry found the raid was a "search-and-destroy mission"
- No officer, FBI agent, or government official was ever criminally charged
- The $1.85 million settlement was paid equally by the federal government, Cook County, and City of Chicago — an implicit acknowledgment of shared responsibility
