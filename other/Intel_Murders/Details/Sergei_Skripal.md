## Circumstances of Death / The Attack

On March 4, 2018, Sergei Skripal and his daughter Yulia were found unconscious on a bench in Salisbury, England. They had been poisoned with the military-grade nerve agent Novichok, which had been smeared on the front door handle of Skripal's home at 47 Christie Miller Road.

Both were rushed to Salisbury District Hospital in critical condition. Detective Sergeant Nick Bailey, who attended the scene, also fell seriously ill. All three ultimately survived after weeks of intensive care. The Skripals were discharged in April and May 2018 and have since been living under new identities provided by British intelligence.

Approximately four months later, on June 30, 2018, Dawn Sturgess's partner Charlie Rowley found what he believed was a discarded perfume bottle in a charity bin in Salisbury. The bottle — a counterfeit Nina Ricci "Premier Jour" perfume bottle — had been used by the GRU operatives to smuggle the Novichok nerve agent into the UK. Rowley gave it to Sturgess as a gift. On the morning of June 30, after Rowley assembled the bottle and spilled some on his hands, Sturgess sprayed the substance on her wrists. She collapsed within 15 minutes.

Dawn Sturgess, a 44-year-old mother of three, died on July 8, 2018, at Salisbury District Hospital. Rowley was also hospitalized but survived.

## Background

Sergei Skripal was a former colonel in Russia's GRU (military intelligence) who had been recruited as a double agent by MI6 in the mid-1990s. He passed the identities of Russian intelligence agents working undercover in Europe to British intelligence. In 2004, he was arrested by Russia's FSB and convicted of high treason in 2006, receiving a 13-year sentence.

In 2010, Skripal was released as part of a spy swap — the largest since the Cold War — in which ten Russian sleeper agents arrested in the US (including Anna Chapman) were exchanged for four Western agents held in Russia. Skripal settled in Salisbury, where he lived quietly until the attack.

Dawn Sturgess was a completely innocent bystander with no connection to intelligence operations. She was a mother of three who struggled with addiction issues and was living in a hostel at the time of her death.
