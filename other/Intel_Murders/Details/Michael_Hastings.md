## Circumstances of Death

On June 18, 2013, at approximately 4:25 a.m., Hastings' 2013 Mercedes C250 Coupe was traveling at extremely high speed southbound on Highland Avenue in the Hancock Park neighborhood of Los Angeles when it struck a palm tree and exploded into flames. Hastings died instantly from massive blunt force trauma. The explosion was so intense that the engine was ejected from the vehicle and found approximately 150 feet from the crash site.

Surveillance video captured the Mercedes running a red light at high speed moments before impact. Witnesses reported hearing the car's engine at full throttle before the crash.

Hours before the crash, Hastings sent an email to colleagues at BuzzFeed stating he was working on a "big story" and needed to go "off the radar" for a while, citing concerns that federal authorities were interviewing his close friends and associates. He also contacted WikiLeaks attorney Jennifer Robinson that night. According to reporting by L.A. Weekly, Hastings had asked to borrow his neighbor's Volvo because he suspected his own car's computer system had been tampered with.

## Background

Michael Hastings was one of the most consequential journalists of his generation. His 2010 Rolling Stone article "The Runaway General" — a profile of General Stanley McChrystal that documented widespread contempt for civilian leadership among McChrystal's staff — led directly to McChrystal's resignation as commander of NATO forces in Afghanistan. Hastings received the George Polk Award for the piece.

At the time of his death, Hastings was a reporter for BuzzFeed and was reportedly investigating CIA Director John Brennan. His last published article, "Why Democrats Love to Spy On Americans," appeared on BuzzFeed on June 7, 2013, and examined the Obama administration's surveillance apparatus.
