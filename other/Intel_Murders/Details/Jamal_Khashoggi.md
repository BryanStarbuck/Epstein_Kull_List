## Assessment: CONFIRMED

The US Central Intelligence Agency concluded that Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman (MBS) ordered the operation to capture or kill Khashoggi. A 15-member Saudi team that included members of MBS's elite personal protective detail carried out the killing inside the Saudi consulate. Turkish intelligence had bugged the consulate and recorded the entire operation.

## Circumstances of Death

On October 2, 2018, Khashoggi entered the Saudi consulate in Istanbul to obtain documents needed for his upcoming marriage to his Turkish fiancee, Hatice Cengiz. He had visited the consulate on September 28 and was told to return on October 2. Cengiz waited outside.

Inside the consulate, Khashoggi was ambushed by a 15-member Saudi squad that had arrived on two private jets from Riyadh earlier that day. According to audio recordings obtained by Turkish intelligence, Khashoggi was strangled to death. His body was then dismembered by Dr. Salah al-Tubaiqi, a forensic doctor who had brought a bone saw. According to Turkish officials, the body may have been dissolved in acid. His remains have never been found.

His last recorded words, captured on the Turkish intelligence recordings, were reportedly "I can't breathe."

## Background

Khashoggi was born into a prominent Saudi family. His grandfather Mohammad Khashoggi was the personal physician to King Abdul Aziz, founder of Saudi Arabia, and his uncle Adnan Khashoggi was a billionaire arms dealer. Khashoggi earned a Bachelor of Business Administration from Indiana State University in 1982.

He had a long career as a Saudi journalist, serving as editor-in-chief of Al-Arab News Channel and as editor of the Saudi newspaper Al Watan. He had close ties to Saudi intelligence and reportedly served as an advisor to former Saudi intelligence chief Prince Turki al-Faisal.

After MBS rose to power in 2017, Khashoggi became increasingly critical of the Crown Prince's authoritarian policies. He left Saudi Arabia in June 2017 and became a columnist for The Washington Post, writing critically about MBS's crackdown on dissent. He also founded DAWN (Democracy for the Arab World Now), a pro-democracy organization.
