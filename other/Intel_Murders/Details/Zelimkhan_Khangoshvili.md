## Assessment: CONFIRMED

A German court convicted Russian national Vadim Krasikov of the murder in December 2021, finding that the assassination was ordered by the Russian state. The court determined that Russian security services provided Krasikov with a false identity, fake passport, and the resources to carry out the killing. Bellingcat identified the operation as being supervised by senior members of the FSB's elite Vympel special forces unit, with planning beginning no later than March 2019.

## Circumstances of Death

On August 23, 2019, at approximately 12:00 PM, Zelimkhan Khangoshvili was walking through the Kleiner Tiergarten, a park in Berlin's Moabit district, on his way to Friday prayers at a nearby mosque. The killer, who had been hiding in nearby bushes, rushed toward him on an electric bicycle and shot him — once in the shoulder and twice in the head — at close range using a 9mm Glock 26 pistol equipped with a silencer.

After the shooting, the assassin sped away along the Spree river for several hundred meters before stopping to dispose of evidence. He jettisoned the electric bicycle, a plastic bag containing the murder weapon, and a wig he had been wearing into the river. Two passersby witnessed him discarding the items and alerted police, who arrested him minutes later at a nearby riverbank.

The killer was carrying a valid Russian passport in the name "Vadim Sokolov," which turned out to be a fabricated identity.

## Background

Zelimkhan Khangoshvili was an ethnic Chechen born in Georgia's Pankisi Gorge. He served as a volunteer platoon commander for the Chechen Republic of Ichkeria during the Second Chechen War, fighting against Russian forces. He subsequently served as an officer in the Georgian military during the 2008 Russo-Georgian War.

After the wars, Khangoshvili allegedly became a valuable intelligence asset for Georgian security services, identifying Russian spies and jihadist operatives on Georgian soil. He reportedly survived at least four previous assassination attempts in Georgia, including a 2015 shooting in Tbilisi.

Facing continued threats, Khangoshvili fled to Germany in 2016 and applied for asylum in Berlin. His asylum application was still pending at the time of his murder. Russian authorities had repeatedly requested his extradition, accusing him of terrorism — requests that Georgia and Germany rejected.

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- A German court formally found the Russian state responsible for an assassination on German soil
- The killer traveled on a state-issued false passport, proving Russian government involvement at the institutional level
- Germany expelled two Russian diplomats in response, and the case severely damaged German-Russian relations

- He had been seeking asylum in Germany; the attack demonstrated that no Western country was safe for Russian dissidents
- Russia had previously requested his extradition on terrorism charges — when the legal route failed, they allegedly resorted to assassination
- Krasikov was later exchanged in a prisoner swap in August 2024, returned to Russia, and reportedly met with Putin
