## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Dorothy Kilgallen was the most prominent journalist in America actively investigating the JFK assassination when she died. She had secured an exclusive private interview with Jack Ruby — something no other journalist achieved — and had obtained classified Warren Commission testimony that she published in her column. Her JFK investigation files and notes vanished after her death and have never been recovered.

## Circumstances of Death

On the morning of November 8, 1965, Kilgallen was found dead in her Manhattan townhouse at 45 East 68th Street. She was discovered sitting upright in bed in a room she did not normally sleep in, fully dressed and with makeup still on, reading a book she had reportedly already finished. The medical examiner, Dr. James Luke, found a combination of alcohol and three barbiturates in her system — secobarbital, pentobarbital, and tuinal — but could not determine whether the overdose was accidental, suicidal, or homicidal. No suicide note was found. The death scene was not treated as a crime scene, and no thorough forensic investigation was conducted.

## Background

Kilgallen was one of the most powerful journalists in America. She wrote the syndicated column "The Voice of Broadway" for the New York Journal-American, which ran in over 200 newspapers. She was a regular panelist on the popular CBS television show *What's My Line?* from 1950 until her death. She was a pioneering female journalist who covered major trials including the Sam Sheppard case and the Lindbergh baby kidnapping trial.

After the JFK assassination in November 1963, Kilgallen became one of the few mainstream journalists to publicly challenge the Warren Commission's lone-gunman conclusion, calling it "laughable." She traveled to Dallas to cover Jack Ruby's trial in 1964 and secured an exclusive private interview with Ruby in a closed courtroom — a remarkable journalistic feat. She reportedly told friends she was close to cracking the case wide open.

## Intelligence Connections

* Kilgallen published classified Warren Commission testimony in her column before it was officially released, indicating she had a source inside the investigation
* She reportedly obtained information from Jack Ruby during their private meeting that she described as "the story of the century"
* According to author Mark Shaw (*The Reporter Who Knew Too Much*), Kilgallen's investigation was allegedly tracking connections between Ruby, the CIA, and organized crime
* FBI Director J. Edgar Hoover reportedly had Kilgallen under surveillance due to her aggressive JFK reporting
* Her hairdresser Marc Sinclaire reported seeing her carry a thick file of JFK assassination papers in the days before her death

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Her JFK investigation file — reportedly containing notes from the Ruby interview and classified documents — vanished from her home after her death and has never been recovered
- She was found in a room she did not normally use, fully made up, in a position inconsistent with someone who overdosed
- The combination of three barbiturates was unusual and not consistent with her known prescriptions
- She had told multiple friends, including her agent and fellow journalists, that she was about to "break the case wide open"
- No proper crime scene investigation was conducted
- Jack Ruby died of cancer on January 3, 1967 — just over a year after Kilgallen, eliminating two key sources of information about the assassination
- Her friend and fellow columnist Ron Pataky was reportedly the last person to see her alive and has refused to cooperate with investigators
