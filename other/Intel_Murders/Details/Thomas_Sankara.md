## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Blaise Compaore and nine others were found guilty and convicted in April 2022 of complicity in Sankara's murder. Compaore, who had fled to Ivory Coast, was convicted in absentia and sentenced to life imprisonment. The Burkinabe judiciary confirmed the presence of French agents in Ouagadougou on October 16, 1987, the day after the coup. While France's direct role has not been judicially proven, declassified documents and the circumstances strongly suggest French intelligence involvement.

## Circumstances of Death

On October 15, 1987, armed soldiers led by Compaore's forces attacked the Conseil de l'Entente building in Ouagadougou where Sankara was meeting with his advisors. Sankara reportedly told his colleagues: "It's me they want. Stay here." He walked out to face the attackers and was shot multiple times. Twelve other people were killed in the assault.

Sankara's body was riddled with bullets. He was quickly buried in an unmarked grave. Compaore immediately assumed power and reversed most of Sankara's revolutionary policies.

## Background

Thomas Sankara, often called "Africa's Che Guevara," became president of Burkina Faso (which he renamed from Upper Volta) in 1983 at age 33. In just four years, he implemented radical reforms: mass vaccination campaigns, women's rights legislation, land redistribution, anti-corruption measures, and programs to achieve food self-sufficiency. He reduced government salaries (including his own), sold the government's fleet of Mercedes, and made the bicycle the official transport of government ministers.

Sankara's pan-African rhetoric, his challenges to French neo-colonial influence (Francafrique), and his refusal to accept IMF structural adjustment programs made him a threat to established power structures.

## Intelligence Connections

- French intelligence (DGSE) is widely believed to have backed the coup, as Sankara's policies directly threatened France's economic and political dominance in Francophone Africa
- The Burkinabe judiciary confirmed the presence of French agents in Ouagadougou the day after the coup
- Sankara's widow, Mariam Sankara, formally accused France of masterminding the assassination
- French President Emmanuel Macron pledged declassification of French documents in 2017, but they have never been fully released
- Compaore, who led the coup, was closely aligned with France, Ivory Coast (under Houphouet-Boigny), and Libya
- Libyan leader Muammar Gaddafi reportedly also played a role in the tensions between Sankara and Compaore

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Sankara was eliminated precisely because his revolutionary model threatened French neo-colonial control of West Africa
- France refuses to release classified documents despite presidential promises
- Compaore ruled Burkina Faso for 27 years after the assassination, reversing Sankara's policies and aligning with French interests
- The trial took 35 years to happen, and only after Compaore himself was overthrown in 2014
- Compaore was convicted in absentia while living in Ivory Coast, which refuses to extradite him
- The pattern mirrors other French-backed coups in Africa during the Francafrique era
