## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Kelly's death was ruled suicide by the Hutton Inquiry -- an informal government investigation that replaced the normal legal inquest. However, multiple medical experts have publicly stated that the official cause of death is implausible. Nine leading doctors and forensic experts wrote to The Daily Telegraph calling for a full inquest. Records related to Kelly's death were sealed until 2073. The circumstances -- a weapons expert who challenged the government's case for war dying just days after being publicly exposed -- are deeply suspicious.

## Circumstances of Death

On July 17, 2003, Kelly left his home in Southmoor, Oxfordshire, for an afternoon walk. His body was found the following morning in woods at Harrowdown Hill, approximately a mile from his home. He was slumped against a tree with his left wrist cut.

The Hutton Inquiry concluded he died from hemorrhage caused by cutting his left ulnar artery with a pruning knife, combined with ingestion of 29 co-proxamol tablets. However, almost no blood was found at the scene -- an extraordinary anomaly given the alleged cause of death.

## Background

David Kelly was Britain's foremost expert on biological warfare and one of the world's leading weapons inspectors. He was employed by the Ministry of Defence as a senior adviser on proliferation and arms control. He had served as a United Nations weapons inspector in Iraq from 1991 to 1998 and had made 37 visits to Iraq.

In May 2003, Kelly gave a confidential background briefing to BBC journalist Andrew Gilligan in which he said that the September Dossier on Iraq's WMDs had been "sexed up" -- specifically, that the claim Iraq could deploy WMDs within 45 minutes had been inserted at the insistence of Alastair Campbell, Tony Blair's communications director, against the wishes of intelligence analysts.

After Gilligan broadcast the claim on the BBC Today programme, the government launched an aggressive campaign to identify the source. Kelly was identified and his name was leaked to the press. He was then called to give evidence before the Foreign Affairs Select Committee in a televised hearing that was widely described as humiliating. He appeared visibly stressed and distressed.

## Intelligence Connections

- Kelly was deeply embedded in British intelligence, working closely with MI6 and the Defence Intelligence Staff
- He had extensive contacts with the CIA through weapons inspection work
- He was involved in the debriefing of Soviet defectors on biological weapons programs
- Kelly was one of the few Western experts who had direct knowledge of Iraq's actual (limited) weapons capabilities
- His exposure directly challenged the Blair government's justification for the Iraq War
- British intelligence services had a clear interest in the "sexed up" dossier controversy being suppressed

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- **Medical implausibility**: Multiple doctors stated publicly that it is almost impossible to die from cutting the ulnar artery (not the radial artery). A group of medical experts including a former coroner wrote that the ulnar artery is small and difficult to access, and would retract and clot if cut
- **Almost no blood at the scene**: Paramedics who found Kelly noted remarkably little blood despite the alleged cause of death being hemorrhage
- **No formal inquest**: The normal legal requirement for a coroner's inquest was set aside by Lord Falconer, who instead authorized the Hutton Inquiry -- an informal government-appointed investigation
- **Records sealed until 2073**: Post-mortem records and other documents were sealed for 70 years -- an extraordinary measure
- **Kelly predicted his own death**: He reportedly told UN colleague David Broucher that he would "probably be found dead in the woods" if Iraq was invaded. He told journalist Tom Mangold: "I will be found dead in the woods"
- **Hutton Inquiry criticized**: The inquiry was widely seen as a whitewash that blamed the BBC while exonerating the government
- **Government hostility**: The Blair government's aggressive pursuit and public exposure of Kelly created the conditions for either suicide or a motive for silencing him
- **Toxicology questions**: The level of co-proxamol found was below lethal levels in many studies
