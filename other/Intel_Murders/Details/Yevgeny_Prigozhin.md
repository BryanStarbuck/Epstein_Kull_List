## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

The crash occurred exactly two months after Prigozhin led the Wagner Group's armed rebellion against the Russian Defence Ministry on June 23-24, 2023. Putin had publicly called the mutiny "treason" and "betrayal." Western intelligence agencies and multiple media outlets reported the crash was likely orchestrated — The Wall Street Journal cited sources saying the operation was overseen by Nikolai Patrushev, head of the Russian Security Council, with a bomb placed under the wing during pre-flight checks. Putin himself later acknowledged that hand grenade fragments were found in the victims' bodies.

## Circumstances of Death

On August 23, 2023, an Embraer Legacy 600 business jet (registration RA-02795) carrying ten people departed Moscow's Sheremetyevo airport bound for St. Petersburg. Approximately 30 minutes into the flight, at an altitude of roughly 28,000 feet, the aircraft broke apart and crashed near the village of Kuzhenkino in Tver Oblast, about 100 kilometers north of Moscow.

All ten people on board were killed, including Prigozhin, Wagner co-founder and military commander Dmitry Utkin, and Wagner logistics chief Valery Chekalov — the three key leaders of the Wagner Group.

Flight tracking data showed the plane made sudden, erratic movements before plunging to the ground. Video footage from the ground captured the aircraft falling with one wing apparently missing. Western intelligence services reported that an explosion likely caused the crash, consistent with a bomb placed on the aircraft.

On October 5, 2023, Putin stated that hand grenade fragments had been found in the bodies of the victims, but he attributed this to grenades the passengers were carrying rather than a deliberate explosive device — a claim widely dismissed.

## Background

Yevgeny Prigozhin was a Russian oligarch known as "Putin's Chef" for his catering company that held Kremlin contracts. He founded the Wagner Group, a private military company that operated across Africa, the Middle East, and Ukraine, becoming Russia's most prominent paramilitary force. He also reportedly founded the Internet Research Agency, the troll farm that conducted disinformation operations targeting the 2016 US presidential election.

On June 23-24, 2023, Prigozhin led Wagner forces in an armed mutiny, marching on Moscow in protest against Russian Defence Minister Sergei Shoigu and Chief of General Staff Valery Gerasimov. Wagner forces captured the Russian military headquarters in Rostov-on-Don and advanced to within 200 kilometers of Moscow before a deal was brokered by Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko.

Under the deal, Prigozhin and Wagner forces were to relocate to Belarus, and criminal charges against him would be dropped. Putin appeared to accept the arrangement publicly, but the mutiny represented the most serious challenge to his authority in over two decades.
