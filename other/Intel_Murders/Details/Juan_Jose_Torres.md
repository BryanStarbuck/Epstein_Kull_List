## Assessment: CONFIRMED

Juan José Torres was a former head of state murdered in exile as part of Operation Condor — the CIA-backed coordination of South American military dictatorships to hunt and kill political opponents across borders. His assassination occurred weeks after Argentina's March 1976 military coup and was carried out with the alleged acquiescence of both the Argentine junta and Bolivian dictator Hugo Banzer.

## Circumstances of Death

On June 2, 1976, Torres was kidnapped from his home in Buenos Aires by armed men. His body was found under a bridge approximately 100 kilometers east of Buenos Aires. He had been shot to death. The killing occurred in the same period as the murders of Uruguayan exiles [Zelmar Michelini](Zelmar_Michelini.md) and [Héctor Gutiérrez Ruiz](Hector_Gutierrez_Ruiz.md), all part of the same wave of Operation Condor assassinations targeting exiled political figures in Argentina.

## Background

Torres served as the 50th president of Bolivia from October 1970 to August 1971. A left-leaning military leader, he nationalized tin mines, expelled the US Peace Corps, and established close ties with labor unions and the Bolivian left. He was overthrown in a coup by General Hugo Banzer in August 1971, reportedly with CIA backing. Torres fled into exile, first to Peru, then Chile, and finally Argentina after the 1973 Chilean coup. When General Jorge Videla seized power in Argentina's March 1976 coup, Torres became trapped — a leftist ex-president surrounded by hostile military dictatorships.

## Intelligence Connections

* Operation Condor — the coordinated intelligence network of South American dictatorships — allegedly facilitated the killing through cross-border cooperation
* The Argentine military junta, which had seized power in March 1976, controlled the territory where Torres was killed
* Bolivian dictator Hugo Banzer reportedly acquiesced in or ordered the assassination of his political rival
* The CIA backed Operation Condor and maintained relationships with all the participating intelligence services
* Declassified US documents have confirmed CIA knowledge of Condor's assassination operations

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Torres was a former head of state — his assassination demonstrated that no exile was safe under Operation Condor
- He was killed within weeks of Argentina's military coup, alongside other prominent South American political exiles
- The coordination between Argentine and Bolivian intelligence to eliminate a shared political enemy exemplifies Condor's cross-border assassination model
- The killing of a former president on foreign soil was an extreme escalation of Condor operations
- US intelligence was allegedly aware of Condor's operations but failed to intervene
