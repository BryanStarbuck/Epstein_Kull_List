# Serena Shim
Lebanese-American journalist for Press TV who died in a suspicious car crash in Turkey in 2014, two days after reporting that Turkish intelligence (MIT) had accused her of espionage for her coverage of ISIS fighters using UN World Food Programme trucks to cross the Syria-Turkey border.

## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

Shim died in a collision with a cement mixer just two days after publicly reporting on live television that Turkish intelligence had accused her of espionage. Her reporting had documented ISIS militants using UN World Food Programme trucks to cross into Syria — a deeply embarrassing revelation for Turkey, which was publicly denying any support for ISIS. The timing of the accusation followed by her death, combined with Turkey's failure to conduct a transparent investigation and the U.S. State Department's failure to contact her family, has fueled suspicion. However, some journalists have noted that a staged traffic collision is an unusual assassination method.

## Circumstances of Death

On October 19, 2014, Serena Shim was traveling in a car near Suruc, Turkey, close to the Syrian border, when a cement mixer truck collided with the vehicle. Shim died at the scene or shortly thereafter. Her camerawoman, who was driving, survived.

The crash occurred while Shim was covering the Siege of Kobane — the battle between ISIS and Kurdish forces for control of the Syrian border town. Turkish authorities ruled the death a traffic accident. The driver of the cement mixer was reportedly briefly detained and released. A Turkish court later found Shim's camerawoman to be the "sole culprit" in the crash.

Press TV deemed the circumstances of her death "suspicious." Shim's mother, Judy Poe, told Fox News she "absolutely" suspected foul play and said her daughter's death was "no accident." The U.S. State Department reportedly did not contact the family following Shim's death, despite her being an American citizen.

## Background

Serena Shim was born in Michigan and raised in the Detroit-area communities of Dearborn and Livonia. Her parents were Lebanese immigrants. She worked as a foreign correspondent for Press TV, Iran's English-language state television network, and had been covering the Syrian civil war and related conflicts from the Turkey-Syria border region.

In the days before her death, Shim reported having obtained images of ISIS and other militant fighters being transported across the Turkey-Syria border in trucks bearing United Nations World Food Programme markings. She documented the presence of ISIS and al-Qaeda-affiliated groups operating freely along the border, and reported on Turkey's alleged role in facilitating the flow of fighters and weapons into Syria.
