## Assessment: CONFIRMED

The UN Special Tribunal for Lebanon spent 15 years investigating and convicted Hezbollah operative Salim Ayyash of carrying out the assassination. However, the tribunal stated it found "no evidence that the Hezbollah leadership had any involvement" and "no direct evidence of Syrian involvement," despite widespread belief that Syrian intelligence directed the operation. The scale of the bombing — equivalent to approximately 1,000 kg of TNT — and its political consequences point to a state-level operation.

## Circumstances of Death

On 14 February 2005, at approximately 12:55 p.m., a massive bomb detonated as Hariri's motorcade passed near the St. George Hotel on the Beirut seafront. The explosion, equivalent to roughly 2,200 pounds of TNT, killed Hariri and 21 others, including his bodyguards and bystanders, and wounded over 200 people. The blast left a crater approximately 30 feet wide and 6 feet deep. Hariri's armored vehicle was destroyed. The attack was one of the largest bombings in Beirut's history.

## Background

Rafik Hariri was a self-made billionaire who made his fortune in construction in Saudi Arabia before entering Lebanese politics. He served as Prime Minister of Lebanon from 1992 to 1998 and again from 2000 to 2004. He was a dominant political figure who led Lebanon's post-civil war reconstruction. Hariri had grown increasingly critical of Syrian influence in Lebanon, particularly the extension of pro-Syrian President Emile Lahoud's term in 2004, which Hariri opposed. He resigned as prime minister in October 2004 and was building an anti-Syrian political coalition when he was killed.
