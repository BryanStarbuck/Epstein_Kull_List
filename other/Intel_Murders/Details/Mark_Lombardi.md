## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

Mark Lombardi died the day before his 49th birthday, at the peak of his artistic career, by hanging in his apartment. His work — large-scale drawings mapping connections between intelligence agencies, banks, organized crime, and political figures — represented a unique form of investigative journalism through art. His death came as he was gaining major recognition, and his computer hard drives were reportedly never recovered. The FBI's interest in his work after 9/11 confirmed the intelligence value of his research.

## Circumstances of Death

On March 22, 2000, Lombardi was found hanging from a sprinkler pipe in his Williamsburg, Brooklyn apartment. A bottle of champagne was nearby, though friends noted he was not a champagne drinker. The apartment was bolted from the inside. Police ruled it a suicide. Standard procedures to establish time of death were reportedly not followed at the scene, and the police report did not note several unusual aspects of the scene. His computer hard drives — containing the extensive database of thousands of index cards documenting connections between covert financial networks — were reportedly never recovered.

## Background

Lombardi was a neo-conceptual artist who created large-scale pencil drawings he called "narrative structures." After working as an assistant curator at the Museum of Contemporary Art in Houston, he began obsessively researching financial scandals of the 1980s and 1990s. Using thousands of handwritten index cards, he tracked connections between banks, intelligence agencies, corporations, and political figures involved in fraud, money laundering, and covert operations.

His drawings documented the Bank of Credit and Commerce International (BCCI), the Iran-Contra affair, the Savings and Loan scandal, Harken Energy, and the World Finance Corporation. One notable work, *BCCI-ICIC & FAB, 1972-91*, mapped the web connecting the CIA, Saudi intelligence, Pakistani ISI, and the bin Laden family through the corrupt BCCI bank. His art was gaining serious attention — he had exhibited at major galleries and was on the cusp of career-defining recognition.

## Intelligence Connections

* His drawings mapped CIA financial networks including BCCI — a bank used by the CIA for covert operations worldwide
* His work documented Iran-Contra money flows linking Oliver North, the NSC, arms dealers, and drug traffickers
* On October 17, 2001 — five weeks after 9/11 — an FBI agent contacted the Whitney Museum of American Art to request a reproduction of Lombardi's BCCI drawing, which mapped connections between the Bush and bin Laden families
* His research into the World Finance Corporation documented CIA-connected money laundering operations in Latin America
* According to journalist Patricia Goldstone (*Interlock: Art, Conspiracy, and the Shadow Worlds of Mark Lombardi*), his database represented one of the most comprehensive privately assembled maps of intelligence-linked financial fraud

- He died at the peak of his career, when major gallery shows and museum recognition were arriving
