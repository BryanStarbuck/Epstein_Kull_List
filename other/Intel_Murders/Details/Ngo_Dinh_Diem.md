## Assessment: CONFIRMED

The CIA provided financial support, encouragement, and operational coordination to the South Vietnamese generals who overthrew and killed Diem. Declassified documents confirm the Kennedy administration sanctioned the coup, and CIA officer Lucien Conein served as the direct American liaison with the plotters. The Church Committee investigated the case as one of five CIA assassination plots involving foreign leaders.

## Circumstances of Death

On November 1, 1963, South Vietnamese generals launched a coup against Diem's government, beginning with a bloody overnight siege on Gia Long Palace in Saigon. Diem and his brother Ngo Dinh Nhu escaped through an underground passage to Cha Tam Catholic Church in Cholon. The following morning, after being promised safe passage into exile, the brothers surrendered. They were placed in the back of an M113 armored personnel carrier. En route, Captain Nguyen Van Nhung, acting under orders from General Duong Van Minh, shot both brothers with a revolver and stabbed Diem with a bayonet. The generals had reportedly promised Diem "honorable retirement" and exile, but General Le Quang Tung and others lobbied for execution.

## Background

Diem was born into one of Vietnam's noble Catholic families and served as Emperor Bao Dai's minister of the interior in 1933. He became prime minister in 1954 and declared himself president of the Republic of South Vietnam in 1955 after defeating Bao Dai in a referendum. Initially supported by the Eisenhower administration as an anti-communist ally, his regime grew increasingly authoritarian and nepotistic, with his brother Nhu running a feared secret police apparatus. His harsh treatment of Buddhists — particularly the Hue Phat Dan shootings in 1963, when soldiers fired on Buddhist protesters — triggered mass demonstrations and horrified the Kennedy administration. The self-immolation of Buddhist monk Thich Quang Duc on a Saigon street became one of the most iconic images of the 20th century and symbolized the depth of opposition to Diem's regime.

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- Kennedy reportedly approved the coup but was allegedly shocked by Diem's murder, suggesting the killing exceeded authorized parameters
- The generals promised Diem safe passage into exile, then executed him — raising questions about whether assassination was always the plan
- CIA officer Conein was reportedly on-site at coup headquarters throughout the operation
- The coup destabilized South Vietnam, contributing to the escalation that led to full-scale American involvement in the Vietnam War
- A 2018 CIA internal history reportedly revealed a deeper agency role than previously acknowledged
- Diem's murder came just 20 days before Kennedy's own assassination on November 22, 1963
