## Assessment: CONFIRMED

The assassination was planned and executed by agents of Chile's DINA intelligence agency under orders from dictator Augusto Pinochet, confirmed by criminal convictions and declassified US documents. DINA operative Michael Townley was convicted and admitted to planting the bomb on orders from Chilean military intelligence. DINA chief Manuel Contreras and operations director Pedro Espinoza were convicted in Chile in 1993.

## Circumstances of Death

On the morning of September 21, 1976, Orlando Letelier was driving to work at the Institute for Policy Studies in Washington, DC. Ronni Moffitt was in the passenger seat and her husband of four months, Michael Moffitt, sat in the back. At approximately 9:35 AM, as the car rounded Sheridan Circle on Embassy Row, a bomb attached to the underside of the car detonated, lifting the vehicle off the ground.

Letelier was killed almost instantly, with both legs blown off. A piece of flying shrapnel severed Ronni's larynx and carotid artery. She drowned in her own blood approximately 30 minutes later. Michael Moffitt survived with a minor head wound.

## Background

Ronni Karpen Moffitt was a 25-year-old fundraiser at the Institute for Policy Studies (IPS), a progressive think tank in Washington, DC. She had been married to Michael Moffitt for only four months.

The primary target was Orlando Letelier, who had served as Chile's ambassador to the United States, foreign minister, and defense minister under President Salvador Allende before the 1973 CIA-backed coup that brought Pinochet to power. After the coup, Letelier was imprisoned and tortured before international pressure secured his release. He became a vocal critic of the Pinochet regime from his position at IPS.

## Intelligence Connections

- The bombing was carried out by DINA agent Michael Townley, an American-born Chilean operative who worked for Pinochet's secret police
- Townley recruited anti-Castro Cuban Americans from the Cuban Nationalist Movement to assist with the operation
- The assassination was part of Operation Condor, a coordinated campaign of political repression and state terror by South American military dictatorships
- The CIA was aware of Pinochet's control of DINA and had intelligence about Operation Condor but failed to prevent the attack
- Declassified documents show US State Department officials pushed for Pinochet's ouster after the Letelier-Moffitt assassination
- DINA chief Manuel Contreras was a paid CIA asset during this period

## Why This Death Raises Questions

- This was a brazen act of state-sponsored terrorism on American soil, in the heart of Washington's diplomatic quarter
- The CIA had intelligence about Operation Condor and DINA activities but did not prevent the attack
- DINA chief Contreras, who ordered the operation, was simultaneously a paid CIA contact
- Townley was convicted and sentenced but served only 62 months before entering the Federal Witness Protection Program
- The killing of an American citizen by a foreign intelligence service on US soil received a remarkably muted political response
- Ronni Moffitt was not a target -- she was a 25-year-old bystander killed as "collateral damage" in an operation against Letelier
