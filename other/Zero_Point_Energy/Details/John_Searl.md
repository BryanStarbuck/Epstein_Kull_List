## Assessment: Suppressed — Devices Seized, Imprisoned, Research Destroyed

John Searl spent over 60 years claiming to have invented a revolutionary energy and propulsion technology. Whether his devices actually worked as claimed has never been independently verified. What is documented, however, is a sustained pattern of suppression: his house was burned down by arson, years of research documents were destroyed or confiscated, his last working SEG was allegedly seized in 1982, he was convicted of stealing electricity and imprisoned, and he was left bankrupt and destitute. He died in 2018 of pneumonia at age 86, still working with collaborators in California to rebuild his technology.

## Circumstances of Death

John Searl died on December 4, 2018, in San Diego County, California. The cause of death was pneumonia complicated by other debilitating health issues. He was 86 years old. At the time of his death, he was living near the premises of SEG Magnetics, Inc. and working closely with the SEG team led by CEO Fernando D. Morris. His death does not appear suspicious — he was elderly and in declining health.

## Background

### Early Life

John Searl was born on May 2, 1932, in Wantage, Berkshire, England. He spent his formative years in care institutions, including Dr Barnardo's Homes. He suffered a childhood injury that partially impaired his hearing. Between the ages of 12 and 18, he reportedly experienced a mysterious six-day gap with no memory — school records noted he had "disappeared."

Searl later attributed the conceptual foundations of his technology to a series of recurring dreams he experienced during childhood.

### Career

In 1954, Searl was employed at Handley Page as a prototype wireman, wiring control systems for RAF Vickers Bombers. In 1963, he moved to Racal Ltd, again as a prototype wireman, working on naval gun control computers for NATO.

### The Searl Effect Generator (SEG)

Between 1946 and 1956, Searl claimed to have designed and built the Searl Effect Generator — a device using concentric magnetic rings and rollers that allegedly produced continuous electrical power without fuel. The SEG consisted of a circular construction with magnetic rollers rotating around rings, incorporating neodymium (rare earth) magnets. Searl claimed:

- The device required no external power input
- It experienced no mechanical wear
- It could generate scalable, continuous, pollution-free energy
- The first prototype allegedly "levitated by itself through the ceiling of his landlady's house" at 33 Crawley Road

### The Inverse Gravity Vehicle (IGV)

Based on the alleged anti-gravitational properties of the SEG, Searl claimed to have developed approximately 40 large flying discs — called "Levity Discs" or "Inverse Gravity Vehicles" (IGVs). During the 1960s and 1970s, he reportedly demonstrated these craft to crowds. People frequently mistook them for UFOs, particularly around Warminster, Somerset.

Searl claimed that one unmanned IGV flew at speeds up to 2,175 mph and could theoretically reach the moon in one hour. No photographic evidence, film footage, or independent witnesses have been verified for any IGV flight.

### Suppression and Destruction

Searl's story is defined by a sustained pattern of loss and alleged suppression:

- **Sabotage by colleagues:** Associates allegedly stole his research and destroyed documentation
- **Arson (1983):** His house in Mortimer, Berkshire was destroyed by arson. Years of research documents were burned or, according to Searl, "driven away in lorries" to unknown locations
- **Equipment seizure (1982):** Searl's last working SEG was allegedly confiscated. There has been speculation — unverified — that U.S. black operations seized his technology
- **Electricity conviction and prison:** In January 1983, Searl was charged with damaging electricity pylons, nearly causing a blackout in southern England. He was convicted of stealing electricity by bypassing his electricity meter and sentenced to 15 months at Reading Crown Court. He also spent time in Dorchester Prison. Searl maintained that his SEG had been powering his home, making the electricity meter irrelevant — the authorities disagreed
- **Financial ruin:** By the early 1980s, Searl was destitute, divorced, and bankrupt

### Rebuilding with Fernando Morris (2003–2018)

In 2003, Fernando D. Morris contacted Searl after learning of his work. Searl was again destitute following a breakup with a UK support group. Morris assisted Searl, eventually relocating him to San Diego County, California. For the next 15 years, they collaborated on rebuilding and documenting the SEG technology through SEG Magnetics, Inc.

### Post-Death Research

After Searl's death in 2018, Fernando Morris and SEG Magnetics, Inc. continue to pursue research and development of the SEG. As of 2025, the organization has reported progress in materials and magnetization research but has not yet produced a functioning device or entered production.

## Why This Person's Case Raises Questions

- **Arson destroyed decades of research:** The 1983 fire at his Mortimer home eliminated years of documentation — documents were reportedly not just burned but also removed by lorries
- **Equipment seized:** His last working SEG was allegedly confiscated in 1982, one year before the fire and imprisonment
- **Imprisoned:** Searl was convicted of stealing electricity — but if his SEG actually powered his home, the charge itself was a mechanism of suppression. Whether the device worked or not, the imprisonment and conviction destroyed his remaining credibility and resources
- **Pattern of total destruction:** By the early 1980s, Searl had lost his research, his equipment, his home, his marriage, and his freedom — all within approximately two years
- **No independent verification survived:** The seizure of equipment and destruction of documents ensured that no independent verification of Searl's claims was possible after 1982
- **Consistent with broader pattern:** The combination of arson, equipment seizure, imprisonment, and financial ruin mirrors the experiences of [Wilhelm Reich](Wilhelm_Reich.md) (imprisoned, books burned), [Paul Pantone](Paul_Pantone.md) (committed to mental hospital), and [Viktor Schauberger](Viktor_Schauberger.md) (coerced into signing away rights, all documents confiscated)

## The Counterargument

- Searl has never produced a working SEG for independent scientific verification
- He claims to have built 40+ flying discs but cannot produce a single photograph, video, or verified independent witness
- The SEG and IGV claims violate established laws of thermodynamics and known physics
- The electricity theft conviction was based on a bypassed meter — a straightforward criminal act regardless of whether a SEG existed
- Searl's theoretical framework — "the law of the squares" and "magnetic bubbles in motion" — has no basis in mainstream physics
- Multiple independent investigations have found no verifiable evidence supporting his claims
- The recurring dreams as the source of his technology and the six-day childhood disappearance raise credibility questions
- Some researchers in the alternative energy community have questioned whether Searl's devices ever existed at all
