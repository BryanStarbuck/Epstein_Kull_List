## How to Use This List

These books provide sourcing, context, and background for the cases documented in our [Details/](Details/) profiles. When researching a specific case:

1. Check the **Key Connections** sections to find books covering that person
2. Use the books as additional sources when expanding profiles
3. Cross-reference claims across multiple authors for corroboration
4. Note which claims are first-person testimony vs. investigative journalism vs. speculation

Many of these books are themselves evidence in this investigation — their authors were sometimes threatened, discredited, or died under suspicious circumstances after publication.

### Recommended Starting Points

**For investigators new to this subject:**
1. Leslie Kean, *UFOs* — the most credible mainstream introduction
2. Richard Dolan, *UFOs and the National Security State* — the most thorough historical overview
3. Lue Elizondo, *Imminent* — the most significant recent insider account
4. Nick Redfern, *Close Encounters of the Fatal Kind* — the book most directly relevant to this project's focus on suspicious deaths

**For researchers focused on specific clusters:**
- GEC-Marconi deaths: Tony Collins, *Open Verdict*
- Roswell and crash retrievals: Carey & Schmitt, *Witness to Roswell*; Corso, *The Day After Roswell*
- Rendlesham Forest: Pope, Burroughs & Penniston, *Encounter in Rendlesham Forest*
- Government disinformation: Greg Bishop, *Project Beta*
- Reverse engineering and exotic technology: Nick Cook, *The Hunt for Zero Point*
- Frank Olson and CIA programs: H.P. Albarelli, *A Terrible Mistake*
- James Forrestal: David Martin, *The Assassination of James Forrestal*
- Congressional disclosure: U.S. Congress, *Transcript of the Congressional Hearing on UAPs*
