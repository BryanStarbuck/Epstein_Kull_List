The official explanation of cocaine toxicity is deeply inconsistent with the physical evidence and circumstances. ## Circumstances of Death

In the early morning of August 4, 2002, Todd Sees rode his ATV from his home at the base of Montour Ridge in Point Township, Northumberland County, Pennsylvania, to scout deer hunting locations along a mountain trail adjacent to high-power transmission lines. He was expected to return within a few hours.

When Sees failed to return by noon, his family became concerned and notified authorities. A massive search effort was launched, involving search and rescue personnel with dogs, helicopters, local and state police, and an organized search team numbering over 200 volunteers. The entire area from the ridge top through adjacent woods and the family property was explored, including a small pond located approximately 70 feet from the Sees home. Divers and dogs searched in and around the pond without success.

Sees' body was found on August 5, 2002, in an area that had reportedly been previously searched. According to witnesses, the body was found wearing only underwear — though Sees had left home fully dressed in boots, camouflage vest, hat, socks, and pants. The body was reportedly emaciated and white (not bloated), with an expression of horror on the face.

His ATV was found separately on the ridge.

### Autopsy and Toxicology

The autopsy found no signs of external or internal trauma of any kind, and the initial results were inconclusive. Toxicology results showed 251 nanograms per milliliter of cocaine and 3,024 nanograms per milliliter of benzoylecgonine (a cocaine metabolite) in his blood. Northumberland County Coroner James Kelley listed the cause of death as "cocaine toxicity" but notably would not classify the death as an overdose, as the cocaine level found was quite low.

### UFO Reports

A farmer and three fishermen on the Susquehanna River reportedly saw a craft hovering above the power lines at the top of Montour Ridge on the morning of August 4, 2002, around 5:30 AM — the same morning Sees disappeared. These reports were published on UFO websites while police were still waiting for toxicology results.

## Background

Todd Sees was a 39-year-old resident of Point Township, Northumberland County, Pennsylvania. He was an outdoorsman and hunter. There is no indication that Sees had any prior involvement in UFO research or related activities — he appears to have been an ordinary civilian whose death became associated with UFO phenomena due to the sightings reported on the same morning.

- The expression on his face was described as one of horror

- No signs of external or internal trauma were found
- A UFO was reportedly seen hovering over the power lines on Montour Ridge by multiple witnesses the same morning

- Some sources claim federal agencies intervened in the investigation, though police have disputed this
