## Assessment: AT RISK

Graves is the most prominent military pilot publicly advocating for UAP transparency. His organization, Americans for Safe Aerospace, has collected reports from over 1,000 military and commercial pilots, making him a central node in the disclosure effort. His Congressional testimony and public advocacy place him in a position similar to other UAP witnesses and whistleblowers who have reported retaliation.

## Current Situation

Graves serves as the founder and executive director of Americans for Safe Aerospace, the world's largest UAP advocacy organization, which he founded in 2023. He also serves as the first Chair of the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA) Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena Integration Committee, representing over 30,000 members of the aerospace industry. He continues to advocate publicly for pilot safety and UAP transparency.

## Background

Graves graduated from Narragansett Regional High School in Baldwinsville, Massachusetts, in 2004 and earned a bachelor's degree in mechanical and aerospace engineering from Worcester Polytechnic Institute in 2008. He served as a U.S. Navy F/A-18F Super Hornet pilot and T-45C Goshawk instructor pilot, deploying aboard the USS Enterprise in 2012 in support of Operation Enduring Freedom and aboard the USS Theodore Roosevelt in 2015 in support of Operation Inherent Resolve over Iraq and Syria.

Between 2014 and 2015, while serving with Strike Fighter Squadron 11 (VFA-11), known as the "Red Rippers," Graves and his squadron repeatedly observed unidentified objects demonstrating advanced flight capabilities off the coast of Virginia Beach. In one pivotal incident during an air combat training mission in Warning Area W-72 east of Virginia Beach, a pilot in his squadron observed a dark gray cube inside of a clear sphere, forcing two jets to take evasive action to avoid collision.

On July 26, 2023, Graves testified before the House Oversight Subcommittee on National Security, stating that "advanced UAP are a national security and an aviation safety problem." He emphasized that UAP sightings "are not rare or isolated but routine," and that they are "grossly underreported" due to stigma.
