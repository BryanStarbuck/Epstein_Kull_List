## Background

Ann Livingston was an accountant by profession who lived near O'Hare Airport in Chicago, Illinois. She was also an active MUFON (Mutual UFO Network) investigator and researcher.

Livingston published an article entitled "Electronic Harassment and Alien Abductions" in the November 1993 issue of the *MUFON Journal*. The article explored connections between reported alien abduction experiences and electronic harassment phenomena — a topic that touched on both UFO research and allegations of government surveillance technology being used against civilians.

### Men in Black Encounters

Livingston reported multiple disturbing encounters with individuals she described as Men in Black (MIB):

On December 29, 1992, at approximately 7:15 AM, Livingston's apartment near O'Hare Airport was illuminated by an intense silver-white flash. Later that same day, while in her apartment parking lot, she was reportedly accosted by five MIB figures. She described them as "almost faceless" and carrying long, flashlight-like black objects. She was rendered unconscious during the encounter.

These experiences reportedly intensified her UFO research and motivated her to publish her findings on electronic harassment. She had also reported multiple UFO sightings in the period leading up to these encounters.
