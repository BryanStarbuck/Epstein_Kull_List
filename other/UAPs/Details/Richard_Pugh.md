## Circumstances of Death

In January 1987, Richard Pugh, a 37-year-old Ministry of Defence computer consultant, was found dead in his flat. His body was discovered with a plastic bag placed over his head and a rope tied around his body, coiling four times around his neck. There were no signs of forced entry or struggle.

The coroner ruled the death an accident, attributing it to autoerotic asphyxiation — a practice sometimes referred to as "sexual misadventure."

## Background

Richard Pugh was a 37-year-old computer expert who worked as a consultant for the Ministry of Defence. His specific projects remain classified, but his work placed him within the broader network of British defense computing specialists. He was one of at least 25 defense scientists and engineers who died under mysterious circumstances between 1982 and 1990.
