## Circumstances of Death

In early 1987 (sources place the date between February and April), Victor Moore, a 46-year-old Marconi defense engineer, died from a barbiturate overdose. Toxicology results confirmed intentional ingestion of the drugs. The death was ruled a suicide.

What sets Moore's death apart from some of the other Marconi cases is that MI5, the British Security Service responsible for domestic counterintelligence, reportedly investigated his death. This suggests the intelligence services considered the death potentially significant to national security, though the results of any MI5 investigation have not been made public.

## Background

Victor Moore was a 46-year-old defense engineer employed by Marconi, working on infrared satellite detection and tracking systems. Infrared satellite technology was a critical component of both the U.S. Strategic Defense Initiative ("Star Wars") and Britain's independent nuclear deterrent surveillance capabilities. Moore's work on these systems placed him at the intersection of some of the most classified defense programs of the Cold War era.

## Why This Death Possibly Raises Questions
