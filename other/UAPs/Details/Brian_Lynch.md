## Circumstances of Death

Brian Lynch died in 1985. His death was attributed to a drug overdose. After his death, a note was found among his personal effects bearing the words: "Five million from Pentagon for Project Scanate."

Project Scanate was a real classified program — it was a CIA-funded remote viewing research project that began in 1972 at Stanford Research Institute (SRI), which later evolved into the broader Star Gate program. The note's reference to Pentagon funding and Project Scanate suggests Lynch may have had contact with or knowledge of classified psychic warfare research.

## Background

Brian Lynch was reportedly a young psychic and UFO contactee. Details about his early life and background are scarce in the public record.

According to Lynch's sister, Geraldine, approximately one year before his death Brian was approached by an intelligence operative working for a PSI-tech company based in Austin, Texas. Geraldine stated that the operatives told Brian they were "experimenting on psychic warfare techniques" and sought to recruit him for this work.

The connection between UFO contact experiences and government psychic research programs is well-documented. Project Scanate, later part of the Star Gate program, investigated remote viewing and other psychic phenomena for intelligence applications. Several researchers have noted overlap between individuals who reported UFO contact experiences and those recruited for government PSI research.
