## Circumstances of Death

On February 16, 2026, at approximately 6:10 a.m., Carl Grillmair was found shot to death on the porch of his home in rural Llano, California, in the Antelope Valley region of Los Angeles County.

Later that day, sheriff's deputies arrested 29-year-old Freddy Snyder of Llano on charges related to carjacking his own relative and burglarizing a home. During the investigation, authorities linked Snyder to Grillmair's shooting. Detectives do not believe the two men knew each other.

Snyder was charged with multiple felonies, including murder, burglary, and carjacking. His bail was set at $2 million. A motive for the shooting has not been publicly released, though the circumstances suggest it may have been related to the burglary spree.

## Background

Carl Grillmair worked at Caltech as an astronomer and astrophysicist for approximately 30 years, based at the Infrared Processing and Analysis Center (IPAC). He was renowned for his research on the collisions and tidal interactions of galaxies and the search for water on planets outside our solar system (exoplanets).

His colleague Sergio Fajardo-Acosta, who worked alongside him for 26 years, mourned his loss. Caltech released a statement following his death.

Grillmair lived in rural Llano, a small unincorporated community in the high desert of the Antelope Valley. The remote location may have contributed to the vulnerability of his property to burglary.
