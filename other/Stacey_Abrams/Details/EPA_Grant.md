## The Bigger Picture: $27 Billion GGRF

The Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund was a $27 billion program under the Inflation Reduction Act. Three major grant competitions were held:

| Program | Total | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **National Clean Investment Fund (NCIF)** | ~$14 billion | Large-scale clean energy deployment |
| **Clean Communities Investment Accelerator** | ~$6 billion | Community-level clean energy |
| **Solar for All** | ~$7 billion | Solar energy access |

Power Forward Communities received $2 billion from the NCIF track, which distributed approximately $14 billion across multiple recipients.

### Other Problematic NCIF Recipients

| Recipient | Amount | Issue |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Coalition for Green Capital** | $5 billion | Grant directed by **Jahi Wise**, GGRF acting director — who was Coalition for Green Capital's **former employer** |
| **Power Forward Communities** | $2 billion | 6-month-old nonprofit with $100 in revenue; CEO also runs largest sub-recipient |
| **Climate United** | $6.97 billion | Led by Calvert Impact Capital |

## How the Money Was Handled

### The Citibank Parking Strategy

Unlike typical EPA grants disbursed through Treasury's ASAP system, the GGRF used an unusual structure:

- **Citibank** served as **financial agent** under a **Financial Agency Agreement** with the U.S. Treasury
- A **tripartite Account Control Agreement (ACA)** was executed among each grantee, the EPA, and Citibank
- Grantees deposited their **entire award balances** at Citibank and drew down funds as needed
- The ACA permitted EPA to assert exclusive control only under specific conditions: substantial noncompliance or evidence of fraud
- The Biden administration directed entities to start drawing down funds within 21 days and gave 90 days to complete training

Critics allege this was done to:

1. **Reduce federal oversight** — once money is in a bank account, it's harder for a new administration to claw back
2. **Accelerate obligations** — rush funds out the door before the January 2025 transition
3. **Limit transparency** — Citibank accounts are not subject to the same reporting as federal accounts

A former Biden EPA political appointee described the disbursements as **"tossing gold bars off the Titanic"** because officials were allegedly rushing to get money out before Trump took office. Zeldin adopted the phrase, describing his discovery as finding **"gold bars"** hidden at Citibank.

### $539 Million "First Wave" (Announced Feb 24, 2025)

Just days before the EPA freeze took full effect, PFC announced commitments of **$539 million** toward initial investments, including:
- Renovation of a historic hotel in Clinton, Iowa into 53 affordable residences
- Energy cost reduction upgrades to 127 senior housing units in Salem, Massachusetts
- Over $100 million in rural investments
- $75 million from LISC Green for multifamily lending in rural communities

The timing — announcing $539M in commitments days before a freeze — raised further questions about whether PFC was racing to obligate funds before the incoming administration could act.

### House Oversight Report: "The Green New Deal Scam" (Sept 11, 2025)

The House Oversight Committee released a 21-page report alleging:
- **Exorbitant executive salaries** with lavish travel benefits
- **Questionable projects** funded by GGRF recipients: housing for artists, a bar and communal kitchen, emissions-free brewery equipment
- EPA weighted **"equity and environmental justice"** as heavily as financial statements in evaluating applications
- One recipient lacked **"basic capacity to comply with EPA's reporting requirements"**

### EPA's Termination Grounds

EPA Administrator Zeldin's March 11, 2025 termination letter cited:
- **Fraud** in the grant application or administration
- **Abuse** of federal funds
- **Conflicts of interest** (Matusiak's dual role)
- **Self-dealing** (majority of funds flowing to Matusiak's other organization)

### The Judge

Judge **Tanya Chutkan** of the D.C. District Court — the same judge who presided over the federal January 6 case against Donald Trump — granted the initial TRO blocking the grant termination.

### The One Big Beautiful Bill Act

On **July 4, 2025**, President Trump signed the **"One Big Beautiful Bill Act"** (Public Law 119-21). **Section 60002** repealed Section 134 of the Clean Air Act — the GGRF's statutory basis — and **rescinded unobligated funds**.

Key dispute: EPA Administrator Zeldin claimed rescissions also apply to **obligated** funds (already committed grants). During House markup, Environment Subcommittee Chair Morgan Griffith stated the provisions only apply to **unobligated** balances — "if a grant was already given, that would still be going forward."

Approximately **$19 million** in unobligated administrative funds was rescinded. The **$19.97 billion** in previously obligated grant money remains in Citibank accounts, contested in court.

### FBI Investigation

The FBI told Citibank the GGRF grantees were involved in **"possible criminal violations"** including **"conspiracy to defraud the United States."** No charges have been filed as of March 2026.

### Current Status (as of March 2026)

- **~$19.97 billion remains frozen** at Citibank, including PFC's $2 billion
- Funds are frozen but EPA **cannot effectuate termination** under current court order
- The DC Circuit en banc court heard oral arguments Feb 24, 2026; **decision pending**
- Supplemental briefing ordered March 9, 2026 regarding the One Big Beautiful Bill Act
- Case widely expected to eventually reach the **Supreme Court**
- PFC and other grantees have been unable to access funds for over a year
