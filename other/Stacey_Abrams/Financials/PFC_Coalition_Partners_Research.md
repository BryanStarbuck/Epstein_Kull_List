## How Power Forward Communities Is Structured

### Legal Entity

Power Forward Communities, Inc. is a **standalone 501(c)(3) nonprofit** incorporated in Maryland in October 2023 by Ari Matusiak (CEO of Rewiring America). It is **not** a fiscal sponsorship and is **not** a division of any partner organization. It is a separate legal entity with its own EIN (93-2705500), its own board, its own officers, and its own Form 990 filings.

It received tax-exempt status from the IRS in March 2024 -- just weeks before being awarded $2 billion from the EPA in April 2024.

### Organizational Classification

- **IRS Category:** Philanthropy, Voluntarism, and Grantmaking Foundations
- **NTEE Code:** Energy Resource Conservation and Development
- **Address:** 11000 Broken Land Parkway, Suite 800, Columbia, MD 21044 (same building as Enterprise Community Partners headquarters)

### Operating Model

PFC operates as a **grantmaking intermediary**. The $2 billion EPA grant flows to PFC, which then distributes funds to its coalition partners and selected subgrantees for specific programs:

- **Rewiring America** -- designs and deploys single-family home electrification programs for LMI homeowners; serves as "contracting counterparty" for owner-occupied programs
- **Enterprise Community Partners** -- leads multifamily affordable housing decarbonization; manages capital deployment for rental properties
- **LISC** -- provides technical assistance to property owners and developers; manages developer/contractor training programs
- **United Way Worldwide** -- community engagement and outreach, particularly in disadvantaged communities
- **Habitat for Humanity International** -- single-family home retrofits through volunteer network

### Red Flag: Shell Entity Concerns

Critics have noted that PFC had:
- $100 in revenue when it received a $2 billion grant
- Zero full-time employees (per 2024 Form 990)
- Only 6 months of existence before the grant award
- Its CEO (Mayopoulos) working only 20 hours/week
- A going concern flag from its independent auditor
- $2.01 billion in assets but $0 in net assets (all liabilities)

### IRS 990 Financial Data

### Top Compensation (2024)

| Officer | Title | Compensation |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Ari Matusiak | Founder/CEO | $482,545 |
| Aimee Witteman | VP Network | $143,912 |
| Cammie Croft | VP Strategic Operations | $142,530 |

### Role in PFC Coalition

Rewiring America is the **primary programmatic partner** for single-family, owner-occupied home electrification. It designs the programs, selects contractors, and serves as the "contracting counterparty" for homeowner retrofits. Its affiliate, the **Rewiring Community Investment Fund**, received the largest share of early PFC disbursements ($5,028,252 of ~$9.5M total).

### Stacey Abrams Connection

- Hired as Senior Counsel in March 2023
- Left at end of 2024
- Compensation at Rewiring America has **not** been publicly disclosed in 990 filings (she does not appear on the 2024 990 officer list)
- Her organizations (Fair Count, Southern Economic Advancement Project) are listed as PFC pipeline partners
- A PFC director reportedly stated she "played a pivotal role" in securing the grant; PFC later characterized her connection as merely "alleged" in court filings

### Political Connections

- **Ari Matusiak:** Obama White House Special Assistant and Director of Private Sector Engagement (2011-2014); helped shape IRA provisions that created the $27B GGRF
- **Alex Laskey (Board Chair):** Also sits on PFC board
- **Stacey Abrams:** Former Georgia House Democratic Leader; 2018 and 2022 Democratic gubernatorial nominee
- **Dark money origin:** Windward Fund/Arabella Advisors network, which has been criticized as the largest dark money operation in American politics

### Controversies

1. **Self-dealing allegations:** Matusiak helped draft the IRA legislation that created the GGRF, then founded PFC to apply for grants from that fund. Senator Martin Heinrich confirmed: "An enormous amount of the work that Rewiring America did ended up in the climate bill."
2. **No lobbying registration:** Despite acknowledged influence on the IRA, no federal lobbying records show Matusiak or Rewiring America registered as lobbyists
3. **Largest sub-recipient:** Rewiring America's affiliate received 53% of all early PFC disbursements despite being one of five partners
4. **CEO double-dipping:** Matusiak earns $482,545 from Rewiring America while serving as PFC board co-chair

### IRS 990 Financial Data

| Fiscal Year | Revenue | Expenses | Total Assets |
|-------------|---------|----------|-------------|
| **2023** | $110,122,180 | $113,542,808 | $913,170,085 |

Enterprise is a **large, established organization** with nearly $1 billion in assets -- the most financially substantial partner in the coalition.

### Role in PFC Coalition

Enterprise leads the **multifamily affordable housing decarbonization** effort. It manages capital deployment for rental property retrofits and energy efficiency upgrades. Its affiliate, **Enterprise Green Accelerator**, received $73,866 from early PFC disbursements.

### Political Connections

- **Shaun Donovan (CEO):** Obama HUD Secretary for 6 years (2009-2014); Obama OMB Director (2014-2017); served in Clinton HUD; ran for NYC Mayor as Democrat (2021)
- **Phyllis Caldwell (Vice Chair):** Obama Treasury Department, Chief of Homeownership Preservation Office
- Received a $65 million donation from MacKenzie Scott in December 2024

### Track Record with Large Government Grants

Enterprise has decades of experience managing federal housing funds, including Low-Income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC) and Community Development Block Grants. It is one of the most established housing nonprofits in the country. This contrasts sharply with PFC itself, which had no track record.

### Controversies

No major financial scandals identified. The main controversy is the potential **conflict of interest** in having Donovan serve simultaneously as Enterprise CEO and PFC Board Co-Chair, meaning he oversees the distribution of funds to his own organization.

### Role in PFC Coalition

Habitat was designated to lead **single-family home retrofits** through its extensive volunteer network and local affiliate infrastructure. As of end-2024 disbursements, Habitat had received **$0** from PFC -- the only coalition partner to receive nothing in early funding.

### Controversies

No major controversies related to PFC. Habitat is widely regarded as one of the most trusted nonprofits in America. The Trump EPA's attempt to terminate the GGRF grants led to headlines like "Trump's EPA Is Trying to Seize Billions From Habitat for Humanity and United Way" -- leveraging Habitat's reputation.

|-----------|--------|
| **Total Revenue** | $373,059,143 |

| **Total Assets** | $1,515,989,183 |

LISC is the **largest partner by assets** -- with over $1.5 billion. It is a major CDFI (Community Development Financial Institution).

### Role in PFC Coalition

LISC leads the **technical assistance** component:
- Provides customized technical assistance to property owners planning decarbonization projects
- Manages developer and contractor training programs
- Provides small grants to offset predevelopment expenses
- Manages a network of technical assistance providers for architects, engineers, and contractors

LISC received **$244,756** from early PFC disbursements.

### Political Connections

- **Robert Rubin (Board Chair for 20+ years):** Clinton Treasury Secretary (1995-1999); Goldman Sachs co-chairman; instrumental in appointing Michael Pugh as CEO
- **Maurice Jones (former CEO, 2016-2023):** Obama HUD Deputy Secretary; Clinton Treasury Department
- **Michael Pugh (current CEO):** Career banker (Carver Bancorp, Capital One, Citizens Financial Group); no direct partisan political connections identified, but appointed by Rubin-chaired board

### Track Record with Large Government Grants

LISC has **40+ years** of experience managing federal community development funds. It is one of the nation's largest CDFIs and has invested over $29 billion in underserved communities. It has extensive experience with New Markets Tax Credits, CDFI Fund awards, and federal housing grants.

### Controversies

No major financial scandals identified for LISC itself. The Robert Rubin connection has been noted by critics as another link to Democratic administrations.

Just days before the EPA freeze took full effect, PFC announced commitments of **$539 million** toward initial investments, including:
- Renovation of a historic hotel in Clinton, Iowa into 53 affordable residences
- Energy cost reduction upgrades to 127 senior housing units in Salem, Massachusetts
- Partner-specific allocations were not disclosed in the announcement

### Key Observations

1. **$2 billion in assets, $9.5M in expenses:** Only 0.47% of grant funds were spent in the first year
2. **Zero full-time employees:** Despite $2B in assets, PFC reported no full-time staff
3. **$0 in net assets:** The entire $2.01B in assets is offset by $2.01B in liabilities (the grant obligation)
4. **Going concern warning:** Independent auditor flagged "substantial doubt" about PFC's ability to continue operating -- presumably because without access to the grant funds, it has no independent revenue

The core allegation is a **closed loop**:
1. Matusiak (Rewiring America CEO) helped draft the IRA provisions creating the GGRF
2. Matusiak then founded PFC to apply for GGRF grants
3. PFC's board is stacked with leaders of the same organizations that receive PFC funds
4. The two board co-chairs (Donovan and Matusiak) each run organizations that are PFC sub-recipients
5. A senior EPA official (Jahi Wise) who oversaw GGRF awards previously worked for another grant recipient
6. Stacey Abrams, a major Democratic political figure, was brought on to Rewiring America to help build the coalition
